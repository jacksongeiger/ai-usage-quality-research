# Measuring "AI-Assisted Engineering Leverage" per Engineer — Findings & Recommended Design

**Mission date:** 2026-07-08 · **Deliverable of:** the July-2026 leverage-measurement research mission (10 survey files, 10 repo reads, 4 adversarial critiques, 5 design candidates, 3 red-team passes, 1 synthesized recommendation — all under `state/leverage/`).
**Conventions:** every external claim carries a URL tagged [primary]/[vendor]/[independent]/[practitioner]; the mission's own reasoning is labeled **[inference]**; anything not verifiable is marked **unverified**. Worked numeric examples with assumed parameters are labeled **[inference — illustration]**.

---

## 1. Executive summary

The current metric — quality-adjusted output per AI dollar, percentile-ranked within job-level cohorts — is beyond repair, not because of tuning, but because two of its four components are structurally dead (a spend denominator whose optimum is *not using AI*, and a CI-clean-rate that saturates at ~1.0) and its assembly commits documented statistical anti-patterns (averaging ordinal percentiles of two ~0.9-correlated ratios). No vendor, framework, or documented org percentile-ranks individuals on output-per-AI-dollar; the only deployed spend-linked individual rankings (Meta "Claudeonomics", Amazon "KiroRank") ran the mirror direction and were gamed and scrapped within months ([Fortune, 2026-07-07](https://fortune.com/2026/07/07/cognition-ceo-tokenmaxxing-big-tech-carried-away-ai-productivity-gains/) [independent]).

**Recommendation: replace it with the AI Leverage Profile (ALP, Anchored Hybrid)** — five non-compensatory facets (Practice · Flow · Quality-floor · Momentum · Experience) plus an unscored cost-showback strip, with **no composite number, no spend denominator, no live peer percentiles, and no fake-neutral defaults**. "Leverage" is honestly re-defined as **practice + trajectory**: (i) a published, calendar-verifiable, FTE-scaled Practice ladder (Stage 0–4); (ii) a self-vs-self Momentum headline (a sentence, not a number) whose top state requires externally-verified output evidence; (iii) quality as a per-repo anomaly *floor* that can only cap, never boost; (iv) all-tool AI intensity (chat + CLIs + agent platform) as unscored context, killing the per-dollar inversion; (v) explicit "unmeasured" states for agent-platform and review-heavy work instead of near-zero scores. The design survived three red-team passes (44 attacks, every one dispositioned in §6), ships v0 on HAVE data alone as SQL + a small batch job, and carries a quarterly validation loop with a committed demotion path so it cannot silently become a vanity adoption score. Its residual weaknesses — practice theater bounded to a private label, invisible review/mentoring work, a promissory quality floor until three cheap metadata acquisitions land — are stated on the dashboard rather than papered over.

## Assumptions (adopted per the brief; the design depends on these)

- Large tech org, O(500–5,000) engineers, GitHub-like hosting; a warehouse (Snowflake-like) already joins usage/PR/CI/HR data per user.
- "AI dollars" = metered per-user spend (API tokens and/or seat amortization), available uniformly across **all** tools including the internal agent-execution platform.
- The agent platform logs per-user spend, tokens, sessions, active days, and (assumed) run counts — but **not** artifacts/outputs.
- The scoring window may be redefined (rolling 8–12 weeks or longer); the current 4 weeks is not sacred.
- Levels ≈ L3–L8; functions: backend, frontend, ML, infra/platform, SRE, data. Cohort redesign is in scope.
- The dashboard may show multiple numbers/subscores; "one score" is not sacred.
- Verify-before-ship data assumptions flagged throughout: per-PR merge timestamps and repo_id exist in the PR warehouse; HR carries an FTE fraction; PR `opened_at` is a near-zero-cost acquisition. **[inference — verify]**
- Threat model: the score is self-view-only, but gaming pressure scales with *perceived* stakes, so every design is evaluated as if it will leak (Amazon told employees KiroRank "would not inform performance reviews"; they didn't believe it and gamed it anyway — [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/07/07/cognition-ceo-tokenmaxxing-big-tech-carried-away-ai-productivity-gains/) [independent]).

## 2. Landscape — framework by framework

Every subsection ends with the mandated red-team pair: (a) how an engineer games it, (b) who it unfairly penalizes.

### 2.1 DORA (four→five keys; AI reports 2023–2026)

**What it measures.** Software-delivery performance of a *team/service*: change lead time, deployment frequency, failed-deployment recovery time (throughput), change fail rate and — new in the 2025 restructuring — rework rate (instability) ([dora.dev four-keys guide](https://dora.dev/guides/dora-metrics-four-keys/) [primary]; [CD Foundation, Oct 2025](https://cd.foundation/blog/2025/10/16/dora-5-metrics/) [independent]). DORA's own words: "These metrics are meant to be applied at the application or service level" [primary]; Nathen Harvey: "These truly are team metrics. And rolling them up organization wide, I think there is a lot of peril to be found there" ([DX podcast](https://getdx.com/podcast/masterclass-on-dora-metrics/) [primary-interview]).

**The AI arc.** 2023 (n>36,000): AI impact "still in its infancy," no performance link claimed ([Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/devops-sre/announcing-the-2023-state-of-devops-report) [primary]). 2024: a 25% increase in AI adoption associated with +7.5% documentation quality, +3.4% code quality, +3.1% review speed — but **−1.5% delivery throughput and −7.2% delivery stability**; 39% report little/no trust in AI code ([Google Cloud 2024](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/devops-sre/announcing-the-2024-dora-report) [primary]). 2025 (~5,000 respondents): 90% use AI; throughput flips positive, **stability stays negative**; central thesis: "AI's primary role is as an amplifier, magnifying an organization's existing strengths and weaknesses" ([dora.dev/dora-report-2025](https://dora.dev/dora-report-2025/) [primary]; [Google Cloud 2025](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/announcing-the-2025-dora-report) [primary]). Companion **AI Capabilities Model**: 7 capabilities incl. "working in small batches" ([Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/introducing-doras-inaugural-ai-capabilities-model) [primary]). 2026 **ROI report** (≈May 2026): ROI framed org-level — "We don't measure AI by the code it writes but by the bottlenecks it clears" ([dora.dev/ai/roi/report](https://dora.dev/ai/roi/report/) [primary]; [InfoQ](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/05/dora-roi-ai-assisted-dev-report/) [independent]). No 2026 "State of…" report existed as of 2026-07-08 ([publications index](https://dora.dev/research/publications/) [primary]).

**Failure modes.** Evidence is survey self-report, cross-sectional, correlational; sample size swung 36k→5k in two years; the headline AI-throughput effect flipped sign in one year — treat any single-year coefficient as unstable **[inference]**. Per-key: deployment frequency counts events not value and is suppressed by release trains; lead time is dominated by wait states and start-point ambiguity; change-fail is a definitional swamp, severity-blind; recovery time is heavy-tailed ("averages can be misleading" — [Octopus guide](https://octopus.com/devops/metrics/dora-metrics/) [vendor]); rework rate depends wholly on planned/unplanned labeling discipline.

**Load-bearing lessons for a per-engineer score [inference]:** (1) individual gains and system outcomes *diverge* under AI (2024's −7.2% stability while individuals feel more productive) — a personal output score can rise while the engineer's system contribution falls; (2) small-batch discipline is an individually-controllable, DORA-endorsed behavior that *inverts* the current lines numerator; (3) the amplifier thesis makes leverage conditional on environment → cohorts must condition on function/environment; (4) percentile-ranking individuals ≈ the league table DORA explicitly forbids. Direct reuse of the keys per engineer is **not possible and not advisable** — we lack deployment/incident/defect linkage, and even with it, a "personal DORA score" is a category error (dead end, logged in §8).

**Gameability & unfair penalty, per key:**

| Key | (a) How gamed | (b) Who it unfairly penalizes |
|---|---|---|
| Deployment frequency | Split work into trivial/no-op deploys; redefine "deployment" (staging pushes, flag flips); rush changes to pump the count | Mobile/desktop/firmware/regulated teams on release trains; monorepo teams with batched deploys; anyone behind a change-approval board |
| Change lead time | **Hide work locally, commit only when done** — Harvey verbatim: "I'm going to do a whole bunch of work over here on the side that's invisible… No, don't do that"; cherry-pick the clock-start definition; skip validation | Engineers doing complex/risky/cross-team work; timezone-split teams (review wait); heavy-CI repos |
| Change fail rate | Definitional drift — reclassify hotfixes as planned follow-ups, record incidents as not-deploy-related, redefine "production" ([cicd.watch](https://cicd.watch/blog/change-failure-rate) [practitioner]); stop shipping anything risky | Honest incident-reporting cultures; high-traffic services where failures are visible; owners of inherently risky surfaces (migrations, payments) |
| Recovery time | Close incidents fast and reopen as "new"; exclude outliers; downgrade severity so the clock never starts | Stateful-system owners (restores are slow by physics); on-call engineers inheriting others' failures |
| Rework rate | Relabel unplanned deploys as planned follow-ups (the same drift lever as CFR) | Teams that document reality; platform teams whose "unplanned" deploys respond to others' incidents |

Meta-point **[inference]**: the keys' gaming levers are almost all *classification* levers, not effort levers — which is exactly why DORA keeps them team-level and target-free. At individual level, every engineer becomes their own classifier, and Goodhart wins immediately.

### 2.2 SPACE

**What it measures.** Five dimensions of developer productivity — Satisfaction/well-being, Performance, Activity, Communication/collaboration, Efficiency/flow — with hard operationalization rules: use **≥3 dimensions**, **≥1 perceptual (survey) measure**, metrics **deliberately in tension** ("this is by design"), and activity metrics "should never be used in isolation either to reward or to penalize developers" ([Forsgren et al., ACM Queue 2021](https://queue.acm.org/detail.cfm?id=3454124) [primary]). SPACE explicitly blesses **opt-in individual self-analytics** ("Developers can opt in to these types of analyses") — the product's exact privacy posture — while requiring anonymized aggregates elsewhere.

**Failure modes.** SPACE itself is doctrine, not validated instrument (an ACM Queue essay). Its named failure — single-metric collapse — is exactly the current design: two correlated per-dollar activity ratios averaged into one number is one Activity dimension wearing two hats, violating ≥3-dimensions, ≥1-perceptual, and metrics-in-tension simultaneously **[inference]**. Its bias guidance (time normalization "would bias against those taking parental leave"; cultural response styles "shouldn't be compared") maps directly onto the current metric's cold-start floors and cross-function cohort.

*(a) Gamed by (activity metrics):* PR/commit confetti, LOC padding — SPACE's stated reason for the never-in-isolation rule. *(b) Penalizes:* engineers whose work is uncounted by activity — reviewers, mentors, agent/infra builders (the precise mechanism behind the brief's agent-platform near-zero scores).

### 2.3 DevEx and DevEx in Action

**What it measures.** Lived friction across three dimensions — feedback loops, cognitive load, flow state — each measured with **both** perceptions and workflow telemetry ("neither alone can tell the full picture"), plus KPIs; break results down "by team and persona — role, tenure, seniority" ([Noda et al., ACM Queue 2023](https://queue.acm.org/detail.cfm?id=3595878) [primary]). **DevEx in Action** is the only quantitative test in the family: PLS on n=219 (9.9% response rate, DX-customer sample, all self-reported outcomes) — flow and cognitive-load hypotheses supported; feedback loops only partially ([ACM Queue 2024](https://queue.acm.org/detail.cfm?id=3639443) [primary]).

**Failure modes.** Survey-only blindness (perception can carry the wrong sign for AI speedups — see METR, §2.7); survey fatigue/non-response (the paper's own 9.9% RR); persona mismatch if results are compared across roles — the direct indictment of the brief's level-only cohort (problem #6).

*(a) Gamed by (survey items):* essentially ungameable when self-view-only (nobody to impress); collectively gameable in both directions when aggregates drive budgets (inflate to please, sandbag to lobby). *(b) Penalizes:* non-respondents; cultural response-style differences; honest pessimists; seniors with higher standards reporting lower satisfaction for identical tooling **[inference]**.

### 2.4 DX Core 4

**What it measures.** Vendor unification of DORA+SPACE+DevEx: Speed (**diffs per engineer — stamped "Not at individual level"** in DX's own table), Effectiveness (DXI, a 14-item survey index), Quality (change failure rate + AI-classified defect ratio), Impact (% time on new capabilities) ([DX Core 4](https://getdx.com/research/measuring-developer-productivity-with-the-dx-core-4/) [vendor]). Shipping mechanics: 12-week lookback, bot PRs omitted, DXI quarterly. Anti-gaming preconditions in their own words: counterbalance with DXI, "not setting targets or rewards," and "It is critical that this metric is never used at the individual level or tied to performance evaluations."

**Evidence quality.** Validation claims (300+ companies, 3–12% efficiency gains, "13 minutes/week per DXI point", Booking.com 16%) are all [vendor], no independent replication found. Will Larson (disclosed DX investor): metric choice "very reasonable," but "skeptical people just don't trust composite metrics particularly well" ([lethain.com](https://lethain.com/measuring-developer-experience-benchmarks-theory-of-improvement/) [practitioner]). AI-era update (Houck, 2026-06-24): "AI doesn't change what engineering organizations are trying to accomplish. What it does is make the signals noisier"; on merge rates: "does a 95% merge rate mean the AI is flawless? Or does it mean your human developers are rubber-stamping machine-generated code?" ([getdx newsletter](https://newsletter.getdx.com/p/revisiting-the-dx-core-4) [vendor]). Microsoft's deployed analogue **EngThrive** pairs North-Star outcome metrics with diagnostic submetrics and a "thriving" guardrail, telemetry + surveys ([arXiv 2605.04259](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04259) [primary]).

*(a) Gamed by:* diffs/engineer via confetti PRs ("extremely easy to game by generating purposeless PRs" — [LinearB critique](https://linearb.io/blog/dx-core-4-deep-dive) [practitioner]); % new capabilities via self/AI-classification wording; DXI via collective survey campaigns. *(b) Penalizes:* platform/agent engineers (non-PR output), reviewers/mentors, maintenance/security/reliability specialists (definitionally "not innovation"), new hires on complex codebases (time-to-10th-PR).

### 2.5 Flow / throughput metrics (cycle time, PR flow, engineering-intelligence style)

**What it measures.** Per-PR time decomposition (coding → pickup → review → merge → deploy), merge frequency, PR size, review turnaround — the metric family every engineering-intelligence vendor and OSS dashboard ships (LinearB, Swarmia, Jellyfish; DevLake and middleware implement it concretely — §3.1, §3.3). LinearB's AI framework pairs a benefits panel (merge frequency, coding time) with an explicit **risks panel** (PR size, rework rate ≤21d, PRs merged without review, review time) ([LinearB](https://linearb.io/blog/ai-measurement-framework) [vendor]) — the clearest vendor implementation of a quality counterweight.

**Failure modes.** Cycle-time is dominated by wait states others control (reviewer latency, CI queues); "faster" conflates rushing with efficiency; unreviewed fast merges read as elite (middleware's `merge_time=0` for no-review PRs — §3.3); Faros telemetry shows the AI-era inversion: individually merged PRs +98% while PR size +154% and review time +91%, with **no company-level throughput/quality gain** — "downstream bottlenecks are absorbing the value" ([Faros AI](https://www.faros.ai/blog/ai-software-engineering) [vendor]).

*(a) Gamed by:* micro-PR slicing; pre-negotiated instant approvals; force-push/rebase to reset clocks; merging trivia to pull medians down. *(b) Penalizes:* cross-timezone teams, rigorous-review cultures, complex-domain owners, big-change engineers — and it charges reviewers' absorbed burden to nobody.

### 2.6 GitClear-style churn/rework and Diff Delta

**What it measures.** Line-level operation taxonomy (added/deleted/moved/updated/find-replaced/copy-pasted), with **churn** = lines "reverted or substantially revised within the subsequent two weeks" ([GitClear 2025 PDF, Appendix A0](https://gitclear-public.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/GitClear-AI-Copilot-Code-Quality-2025.pdf) [vendor]). Headline trends (211M changed lines, 2020–2024): moved code 24.8%→9.5%; copy/paste 8.3%→12.3%; new-code churn 4.78%→7.04%; 2024 was the first year copy/paste exceeded moved code. 2026 report: block duplication +81% since 2023; "long-term update percent" −74%; copy/paste 15.7% in H1 2026 ([Maintainability Gap](https://www.gitclear.com/the_ai_code_quality_maintainability_gap) [vendor]; independent coverage: [LeadDev, 2026-07-07](https://leaddev.com/ai/code-maintainability-plummets-in-the-ai-coding-era) [independent]). The GitKraken×GitClear within-subject study (2,172 developer-weeks): +25% output (Diff Delta) but **churn up to 9x** for heavy AI users ([PR, 2026-03-26](https://www.prlog.org/13135413-gitkraken-research-ai-boosts-developer-output-25but-also-increases-code-churn.html) [vendor]). **Diff Delta** is the one commercial per-engineer durability-weighted output metric: per-line score by operation (Delete 25 pts, Update 20, Add 10, Move/Copy 0) × durability × context filters ([formula page](https://www.gitclear.com/mathematical_proof_of_diff_delta_factors) [vendor]).

**Evidence quality.** Vendor end-to-end, panel composition drifts year-over-year (self-admitted), no causal identification — but directions independently corroborated (they publicly predicted DORA 2024's stability decline before it published), and definitions unusually transparent. Sharpest published objections are HN practitioner comments (churn may no longer proxy quality if revision cost fell; population-mix confounds) ([HN thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=39177008) [practitioner]). Academic anchor: **relative** code churn predicts defect density (89% discrimination) while **absolute churn is a poor predictor** (Nagappan & Ball, [ICSE 2005](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1062455.1062514) [independent]) — any raw per-engineer churn number is noise until cohort-normalized.

**Relevance here:** churn/duplication is the most direct available counterweight to volume numerators — GitClear's data shows copy/paste is exactly how AI inflates lines, so the current lines-per-dollar numerator is maximally exposed to the documented pathology; also <50% of raw changed lines survive their meaningfulness filters. But **none of it is computable from HAVE data** (needs line-level diff history); the metadata-only cousin (per-PR file lists → same-file re-touch ≤21d) is a top acquisition (§7).

**Per-metric gameability and penalties:**

| Metric | (a) Gamed by | (b) Penalizes |
|---|---|---|
| Churn % (lower=better) | Push only "finished" work (defeats CI early-signal); wait until day 15 to fix known issues; fix via adjacent new lines/files; explore on never-merged branches; lobby for squash-merge | Honest iterators/prototypers; volatile product surfaces; diligent post-merge responders to review feedback; trunk-based small-commit teams (workflow determines what churn is *visible*) |
| Copy/paste % & block duplication | Trivial renames to break exact-line matching; spread duplication across commits (detector is within-commit); move boilerplate into excluded generated files | Test authors; data/migration/config engineers; verbose-by-convention languages (Go error handling) |
| Moved % ("refactoring share", higher=better) | Performative cut/paste file reorganization — matches the detector exactly | Greenfield engineers (nothing to move); anyone whose refactors move-and-edit (misclassified as delete+add) |
| Diff Delta (higher=better) | Farm Delete points (25 pts/line) on dead code; plausible small Updates (20 pts) across old files; avoid zero-point moves | Maintainers of stable systems; non-PR/agent engineers; proprietary weights mean an engineer cannot audit their own score — an interpretability failure in itself |
| Survival / half-life (higher=better) | Write code nobody touches (dead code, cold boilerplate); ownership gatekeeping against refactors of "your" files | Fast-evolving-area engineers; prototype/experiment teams; anyone whose correct code is deleted for business reasons — and high survival can be pathology (GitClear's own 2026 "frozen strata" finding) |
| Code Time (active editor minutes) | Keep the editor warm — the 10-minute idle rule is trivially defeated | Engineers who think/design/review/pair/operate rather than type — exactly the seniors and agent-orchestrators |

### 2.7 GitHub Copilot & enterprise studies; independent/academic evidence incl. METR

**Vendor RCTs (greenfield, favorable).** Peng et al. 2023: n=95 Upwork freelancers (≈35 analyzed completers), one greenfield JS task — Copilot 55.8% faster, 95% CI [21%, 89%], task-success effect n.s.; "this study does not examine the effects of AI on code quality" ([arXiv 2302.06590](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.06590) [vendor]). Google internal RCT: ~21% faster on one enterprise task, wide CI, explicit non-generalization caveat ([arXiv 2410.12944](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.12944) [vendor]).

**The acceptance-rate arc.** Ziegler et al.: acceptance rate best predicted *perceived* productivity — ρ=0.24, ~6% of variance; the authors pre-emptively debunked their own metric ("cutting code suggestions into half… would likely increase acceptance rate without… user benefit. We can thus not recommend acceptance rate as singular and ultimate criterion") ([arXiv 2205.06537](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.06537) [vendor]). Mozannar et al.: "Thinking/Verifying Suggestion" alone = 22.4% of session time — acceptance ignores the verification cost the AI creates ([arXiv 2210.14306](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.14306) [primary]). Practitioners then called it a "vanity metric" ([LeadDev, 2025-07-10](https://leaddev.com/reporting/the-rise-and-looming-fall-of-acceptance-rate) [practitioner]), and GitHub sunset the acceptance-centric APIs (Apr 2026), replacing them with engaged-users/feature-mix/LoC-funnel/`ai_credits_used` reporting ([changelog, 2026-01-29](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-01-29-closing-down-notice-of-legacy-copilot-metrics-apis/) [primary]).

**The best causal evidence.** Cui et al. (Management Science 2026; 3 field RCTs at Microsoft/Accenture/Fortune-100, **n=4,867**): pooled merged PRs **+26.08% (SE 10.3)**; commits n.s.; builds +38.4%; **build success rate flat-to-negative (−17.4%, significant, at Accenture)** — "trial-and-error coding"; juniors gain +27–39% vs seniors +8–13%; low-pre-productivity devs accept significantly more suggestions; outcome noise brutal (weekly PR means 0.13–0.87 with SD > mean, "high fraction of zeros") ([SSRN 4945566](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4945566) [primary]). The GitHub/Accenture marketing blog surfaced build *volume* +84% and omitted the success-*rate* decline ([github.blog](https://github.blog/news-insights/research/research-quantifying-github-copilots-impact-in-the-enterprise-with-accenture/) [vendor]; identity with Cui's Accenture arm plausible but **unverified**).

**METR — the boundary condition.** RCT, 16 experienced OSS maintainers, 246 real tasks on mature repos: allowing early-2025 AI made them **19% slower**, while they forecast +24% and still believed +20% afterward ([metr.org](https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/) · [arXiv 2507.09089](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.09089) [independent]). **Feb-2026 update** (57 devs, 800+ tasks, late-2025 models): original cohort flips to −18% (faster), CI −38% to +9%; new devs −4%, CI crosses zero; METR itself calls the new data "an unreliable signal" and "only very weak evidence" because 30–50% of developers refused to submit tasks they'd have to do without AI ([metr.org update](https://metr.org/blog/2026-02-24-uplift-update/) [independent]). Honest state of causal evidence as of mid-2026: **sign uncertain, heterogeneity large, and even the best measurement designs are collapsing under selection effects** **[inference]**.

**Other independent evidence.** Uplevel (~800 devs, observational): no cycle-time/throughput gain; Copilot group **+41% bugs** (bug methodology gated — directional only) ([uplevelteam.com](https://uplevelteam.com/blog/ai-for-developer-productivity) [vendor, no AI-product stake]). Faros "Acceleration Whiplash" (22k devs telemetry): task throughput/dev +33.7% but **bugs/dev +54%**, median review time 5x, **31% more PRs merged without review** ([faros.ai](https://www.faros.ai/research/ai-acceleration-whiplash) [vendor]). Security: ~40% of Copilot-generated programs vulnerable in CWE scenarios ([Pearce, IEEE S&P 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.09293) [independent]); AI-assisted users wrote *less* secure code while *more* confident it was secure ([Perry et al., CCS 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.03622) [independent]); 45% of generations across 100+ LLMs failed security checks ([Veracode 2025](https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/) [vendor]); in-the-wild Copilot snippets: 29.5% (Python)/24.2% (JS) with CWE weaknesses ([Fu et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.02059) [independent]). OSS agent adoption diff-in-diff: static-analysis warnings +18%, cognitive complexity +39% ([Agarwal et al., Jan 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13597) [independent]). Stanford telemetry talks (~100k devs, not peer-reviewed, magnitudes unverified): greenfield ~30–40% gains vs brownfield ~0–10%, sometimes negative ([Denisov-Blanch](https://getcoai.com/video/does-ai-actually-boost-developer-productivity-100k-devs-study-yegor-denisov-blanch-stanford/) [independent]). Systematic review of 39 studies: perceived benefits broad; measured quality outcomes "contradictory… contingent on context" ([arXiv 2507.03156](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.03156) [independent]). Stack Overflow 2025 (n≈49k): AI trust down to 29%; #1 frustration (66%) "almost right, but not quite"; 45% say debugging AI code is more time-consuming ([survey](https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/ai) [primary survey]).

**Convergent finding [inference]:** AI-heavy code ships with more latent defects, duplication, and complexity — **and essentially all of it passes CI**. This refutes CI-clean-rate as a quality adjuster independently of its saturation. And the single most replicated heterogeneity — juniors adopt/accept/gain more — means any adoption- or acceptance-flavored score reads systematically higher for juniors; a dashboard that ranks on it silently tells senior engineers they are bad at AI when METR suggests they may be correctly selective.

**Per-signal failure modes, gameability, and unfair penalties:**

| Signal | Failure mode (evidence) | (a) Gamed by | (b) Penalizes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acceptance rate | ρ=0.24 with even *perceived* productivity; varies by language/time-of-day; inversely related to tenure and prior productivity (Cui); denominator ("shown") controlled by vendor trigger heuristics; incomparable across tools | Accept-everything-then-delete; aggressive completion triggers; boilerplate-heavy files; off-hours work (+2pp free lift per Ziegler); under mandates, literal "accept to comply" | Seniors/high-prior-productivity engineers (empirically accept less); weak-model domains; chat/agent-first users; careful verifiers (their reading time is the cost acceptance ignores) |
| Persistence/retention (30–600s) | Correlates *worse* with perceived productivity than acceptance (ρ≤0.07, Ziegler); attribution decays as code becomes "simply part of the code"; vendor-only | Accept only trivially-obvious completions; never revise generated code even when it should be | Refactorers and rapid iterators (legitimate edits count as non-persistence) |
| PR/commit/build counts | Zero-inflated, SD>mean; underpowered even at n≈5,000 over months (Cui) → a 4-week individual window is statistically hopeless | PR-splitting (same shape as Ziegler's suggestion-splitting warning); trivial dep-bump PRs; commit spam; compile-looping (builds +38% "for free") | Non-PR roles (agent builders, infra, ML experimentation); long-cycle work; big-PR-norm functions; heavy-CI monorepo residents |
| Build/CI success rate | Declined significantly at Accenture while volume rose; "trial-and-error coding" makes builds cheap; matches the client's observed saturation | Iterate until green before pushing; push only safe changes; avoid flaky-CI repos | Risky/hard-change owners; 50+-check repos; early-feedback draft-PR users |
| Self-reported time savings | METR: felt +20%, measured −19%; Peng's subjects guessed 35% vs measured 55.8% — miscalibrated in both directions | Costless optimism; anchoring on vendor marketing numbers | Honest skeptics and experts whose true gains are genuinely small — the best-calibrated people read as "bad at AI" |
| Engaged/active users, feature flags | Presence, not value; GitHub distinguishes "active" vs "engaged" because raw activity over-counts | One trivial prompt per day; opening agent mode once a month ticks the box | Part-timers, on-call weeks, meeting-heavy senior roles, leave-takers |
| LoC suggested/added; "% code by AI"; credits | Rewards verbose generation; addition-only counts mislead (GitHub now ships deletes separately) | Verbose prompting; agent whole-file rewrites; credit burn (if read as engagement) or hoarding (if read as cost) | Deleters/simplifiers; low-token high-judgment work; cheaper-model users |

### 2.8 "Goodput" / quality-adjusted throughput — and the per-dollar / ROI / FinOps framing

**Goodput.** The term (useful throughput net of retransmissions/overhead) has **no OSS engineering implementation anywhere** — it exists only in LLM-serving benchmarks ([vLLM issue](https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm/issues/8782) [primary]) and Google's ML-training "ML Productivity Goodput" ([Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/goodput-metric-as-measure-of-ml-productivity) [vendor]). Anyone claiming an off-the-shelf engineering-goodput library is bluffing (verified dead end). The nearest real implementations are GitClear's Diff Delta (§2.6) and revert/rework netting from PR metadata (middleware's `revert-<pr#>` detection, §3.3). The mission built a goodput design candidate anyway (§5, NDG) — its honest verdict: defensible only after path-exclusion and revert/rework acquisitions; a HAVE-only version is log-tempered LOC, i.e., the commodity-gameable quantity.

**Per-dollar / ROI.** The industry holds two contradictory theories of engineer AI spend. *Spend-as-investment:* Huang: "If that $500,000 engineer did not consume at least $250,000 worth of tokens, I am going to be deeply alarmed"; Databricks applauded a $7k-in-two-weeks engineer; Sendbird ranks token spenders "Beginner"→"AI God" ([Forbes, 2026-03-31](https://www.forbes.com/sites/richardnieva/2026/03/31/the-ai-gods-spending-as-much-as-they-can-on-ai-tokens/) [independent]). *Spend-as-cost:* FinOps Foundation recommends unit costs and **showback at workload/project/team level — no per-person efficiency metrics** ([finops.org](https://www.finops.org/wg/finops-for-ai-overview/) [primary]); Gartner predicts AI coding costs surpass the average developer salary by 2028 and recommends governance, not scoring ([press release](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-06-24-gartner-predicts-ai-coding-costs-will-surpass-average-developer-salary-by-2028-as-token-consumption-surges) [primary]; [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/24/ai-coding-agents-could-soon-cost-more-than-the-developers-using-them/5260864) [independent]).

**The decisive data.** Jellyfish (12k devs, 200 companies, Q1 2026): bottom-20% token spenders — 11 merged PRs/quarter at ~$3 (**$0.28/PR**); top-20% — 23 PRs at ~$1,822 (**$89.32/PR**). Heavy users are ~2x as productive on ~10x the tokens; a per-dollar ratio mechanically ranks the org's demonstrably most productive engineers **last**; Jellyfish's own advice is "move the middle," not squeeze the heavy users ([Jellyfish, 2026-04-15](https://jellyfish.co/blog/is-tokenmaxxing-cost-effective-new-data-from-jellyfish-explains/) [vendor]). Independently: on identical SWE-bench tasks, token cost varies up to **30x run-to-run**, cost only weakly tracks expert-rated difficulty, and accuracy peaks at intermediate cost ([Bai, Mihalcea, Brynjolfsson et al., arXiv 2604.22750](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22750) [independent]) — spend is noise about both difficulty and waste. Deployed precedent: Meta's "Claudeonomics" (85k staff; idle agents run to inflate rank) and Amazon's "KiroRank" (fabricated tasks; "tokenmaxxing" was Amazon's internal term) were both scrapped; Cognition CEO Scott Wu: "let's try and rank people by how much output they're actually producing" ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/07/07/cognition-ceo-tokenmaxxing-big-tech-carried-away-ai-productivity-gains/) [independent]; [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/meta-employees-compete-for-token-consumption-on-an-internal-ai-leaderboard/) [independent, secondhand]). **No documented case of any org percentile-ranking individuals on output-per-AI-dollar was found** despite extensive search — the client's design is empirically untested everywhere (absence of evidence, stated plainly; unverified that none exists privately). Downstream-cost framing: Entelligence claims only $0.18 of every AI dollar reaches shipped product ($0.44 bug-fixing, $0.27 rework) — vendor with strong COI, no pre-AI baseline, directional only ([research.entelligence.ai](https://research.entelligence.ai/) [vendor]).

*(a) Gamed by (any per-dollar score):* suppress the denominator — personal/free accounts, unmetered chat tools, the org's own agent platform, cheapest-model theater, prompt starvation with invisible quality cost; slice numerators. *(b) Penalizes:* heavy legitimate spenders on hard problems; agent-platform builders (spend, no PR output); large-context monorepo engineers (input tokens dominate cost); the org's most productive adopters (Jellyfish inversion).

### 2.9 Vendor AI-measurement frameworks (DX, Jellyfish, LinearB, Swarmia, Uplevel, Faros, Athenian, Waydev)

**The universal pattern:** a **Utilization → Impact → Cost funnel measured at team/cohort level**. No vendor publishes a per-engineer "AI leverage" score and none uses a per-dollar-per-engineer denominator — every one either aggregates cost at org level or explicitly warns against individual performance use (survey of all eight vendors, 2026-07-08). Highlights: DX — adoption cohorts, % AI-assisted PRs via proprietary "file system-level observability," self-reported time savings (2.4 hrs/wk default → "39x ROI" marketing math), explicit warning that "metrics like code generation volume are particularly susceptible to gaming" ([implementation guide](https://getdx.com/blog/how-to-implement-ai-measurement-framework/) [vendor]). Jellyfish — AI users vs non-users cycle-time deltas, token cost by team/initiative; no statistical methodology disclosed (self-selection-confounded **[inference]**) ([jellyfish.co](https://jellyfish.co/platform/jellyfish-ai-impact/) [vendor]). LinearB — declarative AI labels + benefits/risks panels ([linearb.io](https://linearb.io/blog/ai-measurement-framework) [vendor]). Swarmia — the most transparent detection docs, including an admitted over-reporting 24-hour heuristic, and the best **agent-metrics template** (agent PRs, merge %, human-intervention share) ([help.swarmia.com](https://help.swarmia.com/features/ai-tools/ai-tool-detection-and-filters.md) [vendor]). Uplevel — team-level only by design. Faros — cohort/before-after deltas, team-level dashboards, "Token Intelligence" classifying tokens productive/inefficient/wasteful at **team** grain ([faros.ai](https://www.faros.ai/blog/token-intelligence-for-ai-engineering) [vendor]). Athenian — refused individual scoring on principle ("engineers hate them because they feel like surveillance software" — [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2022/03/02/athenian-gives-you-metrics-about-your-engineering-team-without-focusing-on-individuals/) [independent]). Waydev — naive before/after; weakest methodology **[inference]**.

**Cross-vendor failure modes:** AI attribution unsolved (labels are declarative, trailers strippable/spoofable, heuristics over-report); users-vs-non-users deltas self-selection-confounded ("early adopters tend to be high performers, skewing results" — Swarmia's own words); self-reported time savings anchor ROI and are unverifiable; speed metrics without quality counterweights invert under AI (Faros bugs +54%; Uplevel +41%).

**Per-signal-family gameability and penalties (cross-vendor):**

| Signal family (vendors) | (a) Gamed by | (b) Penalizes |
|---|---|---|
| DAU/WAU, active days, power-user badges (DX, Jellyfish, Faros "15 days/30 hours" rule) | Open the tool daily, fire trivial prompts; leave sessions running | Batch deep-workers; part-time/on-call weeks; tools that under-report telemetry |
| Acceptance rate, LoC suggested/accepted (Jellyfish, LinearB, Faros) | Accept everything then rewrite; prompt for verbose boilerplate | Careful suggestion reviewers; weak-model domains; deleters/refactorers |
| % AI-generated code / AI-PR labels incl. 24h heuristic (DX, Swarmia, LinearB) | Regenerate barely-changed files; spoof or strip one-line `Made-with:` trailers; one AI touch/day makes ALL your PRs "AI-assisted" (Swarmia's admitted over-report) | Architects/reviewers/mentors whose value isn't committed code; honest non-labelers |
| Self-reported time savings (DX 2.4 hrs/wk anchor) | Inflate hours; copy the vendor's anchor | Honest reporters; skeptics score their org's ROI down |
| Cycle-time/merge-frequency deltas (all) | Micro-PRs; offline pre-negotiated reviews; merge trivia | Complex-domain and heavy-CI teams; multi-timezone review chains; non-PR work |
| Rework rate ≤21d (LinearB) | Defer fixes past day 21; avoid touching own recent code | Fast iterators; legitimate refactoring; hotfix-heavy teams |
| Bugs/incidents per developer (Faros, Uplevel) | Under-file bugs; reclassify severity | Rigorous-triage cultures; owners of bug-dense legacy code |
| Org ROI = hrs×salary÷cost (DX) | Inflate numerator surveys; ration licenses to suppress the denominator | Heavy spenders doing legitimately harder work — the client's own per-dollar flaw at org scale |

### 2.10 Agent-native measurement (agents, platforms, automation, non-PR work)

**Headline honesty statement (verified across four traditions):** there is **no published, validated methodology for scoring an individual engineer by the value of the agents/automation/platforms they build.** Every credible framework measures (a) the *agent's* runtime performance, (b) the *platform's* org-level ROI, or (c) treats agents as team extensions and punts on individual attribution. OpenTelemetry GenAI semantic conventions capture errors/latency/tokens/call-structure but have **no notion of task success or output value** ([spec repo](https://github.com/open-telemetry/semantic-conventions-genai) [primary]); Langfuse-class observability defines "success" only as a builder-authored score ([langfuse docs](https://langfuse.com/docs/evaluation/overview) [vendor/OSS]). Google Cloud's agent-KPI framework (Feb 2026) is the most concrete: cost-per-successful-task ("$0.10/run with 50% failure = $0.20 actual cost per success"), implicit rejection via undo/revert tracking — all agent/org-level, never the builder ([cloud.google.com](https://cloud.google.com/transform/the-kpis-that-actually-matter-for-production-ai-agents) [vendor]). DX explicitly defers agent-specific metrics as premature ([getdx.com](https://getdx.com/research/measuring-ai-code-assistants-and-agents/) [vendor]; its "human-equivalent agent hours" was not found on the fetched page — **unverified, do not build on it**). Platform value precedents: Spotify Backstage's north star was time-to-10th-PR (~60→<20 days) plus plugin-ecosystem adoption (~85% of contributions from outside the platform team) ([InfoQ 2023](https://www.infoq.com/news/2023/04/spotify-success-backstage/) [independent]); CNCF's platform maturity model builds in a Goodhart warning at top maturity ([CNCF whitepaper](https://tag-app-delivery.cncf.io/whitepapers/platform-eng-maturity-model/) [primary]); DORA 2024's platform chapter shows a J-curve — platform investment depresses throughput/stability before it pays off (figures cross-reported; verify against the PDF) — direct evidence that delivery metrics **mis-price platform work in flight** ([dora.dev 2024](https://dora.dev/research/2024/dora-report/) [primary]). UiPath's Automation Hub is the reference implementation *and cautionary tale* for hours-saved: every load-bearing input is unvalidated submitter self-report ([UiPath docs](https://docs.uipath.com/automation-hub/automation-cloud/latest/user-guide/information-about-the-detailed-assessment-algorithm) [vendor]). The invisible-labor literature diagnoses the fairness failure but offers no telemetry solution ([arXiv 2401.06889](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.06889) [independent]; Tanya Reilly, ["Being Glue"](https://www.noidea.dog/glue) [practitioner]).

**The strongest transferable pattern**, converging across Backstage, CNCF, software-reuse research ([Frakes & Terry 1996](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/234528.234531) [primary, academic]) and agent-KPI frameworks: **adoption/reuse by other people, especially repeat use** — which requires one NEW signal (agent_id + owner_id + caller identity per run) that the platform almost certainly already logs but the warehouse does not expose **[inference]**.

*(a) Gamed by:* run counts — cron self-invocation, workflow splitting (zero marginal cost); distinct-consumer counts — astroturf rings, demo spikes (materially harder: requires other humans); run success — trivial success definitions, kill doomed runs early (the CI-clean pathology one level up); builder-defined evals — write a lenient judge; hours saved — inflate unvalidated inputs. *(b) Penalizes:* builders whose consumers are *systems* (service accounts, schedulers); few-high-leverage-agent builders vs many-trivial-agent builders; rigorous self-evaluators; seasonal/niche tools; new agents in cold start.

### 2.11 The Goodhart / metric-design toolbox (cross-cutting)

Goodhart ("When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure" — Strathern's phrasing) and Campbell's stronger form (the *process itself* corrupts) frame everything above ([Rodamar, Significance 2018](https://rss.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/j.1740-9713.2018.01205.x) [independent]). Manheim & Garrabrant's four modes — regressional, extremal, causal, adversarial — are the red-team checklist used throughout this document ([arXiv 1803.04585](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.04585) [primary]). Austin (1996): under *partial* measurement, effort flows from unmeasured to measured dimensions until the real goal fails; the sanctioned mode is **informational (self-view) measurement** [academic]. Surrogation (Wells Fargo): mitigate with multiple yardsticks, no incentives tied to the metric ([Harris & Tayler, HBR 2019](https://hbr.org/2019/09/dont-let-metrics-undermine-your-business) [independent]). The LOC-gaming canon: Atkinson's "−2000 lines" ([folklore.org](https://www.folklore.org/Negative_2000_Lines_Of_Code.html) [practitioner/primary anecdote]); Dan North's "worst programmer" who scored zero story points while making the whole team faster ([dannorth.net](https://dannorth.net/the-worst-programmer/) [practitioner]); commit fabrication is commodity tooling ([fake-git-history](https://github.com/artiebits/fake-git-history), [gitfiti](https://github.com/gelstudios/gitfiti) [primary artifacts]). McKinsey's 2023 individual-productivity framework drew the definitive rebuttals: Beck/Orosz — "If you choose to create incentives around measures, know that you will never again receive accurate data"; self-measurement for personal improvement is the legitimate use case ([Pragmatic Engineer](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/measuring-developer-productivity), [Beck part 2](https://newsletter.kentbeck.com/p/measuring-developer-productivity-440) [practitioner]); North — measuring individual contribution "is like trying to measure the individual contribution of a piston in an engine" ([dannorth.net](https://dannorth.net/blog/mckinsey-review/) [practitioner]). (A GeePaw Hill McKinsey rebuttal could **not** be verified — do not cite.)

**Statistical toolbox** (used in §4–§6): percentile ranks are ordinal and must not be averaged ([reference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentile_rank)); arithmetic means of normalized ratios give normalization-dependent rankings — use geometric means ([Fleming & Wallace, CACM 1986](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/5666.5673) [primary]); ratio-metric variance needs the delta method ([Deng, Knoblich & Lu, KDD 2018](https://alexdeng.github.io/public/files/kdd2018-dm.pdf) [primary]); Wald intervals are chaotic at small n, use Wilson ([Brown, Cai & DasGupta 2001](http://www-stat.wharton.upenn.edu/~lbrown/Papers/2001a%20Interval%20estimation%20for%20a%20binomial%20proportion%20%28with%20T.%20T.%20Cai%20and%20A.%20DasGupta%29.pdf) [primary]); empirical-Bayes Beta-binomial shrinkage replaces cold-start floors ([Robinson](http://varianceexplained.org/r/empirical_bayes_baseball/) [practitioner]); hierarchical partial pooling solves small-cell cohorts ([Stan case study](https://mc-stan.org/rstanarm/articles/pooling.html) [primary]); winsorize at distribution percentiles chosen by sensitivity analysis, never fixed absolute constants ([SAS caution](https://blogs.sas.com/content/iml/2017/02/08/winsorization-good-bad-and-ugly.html) [practitioner]); composite QA per the [OECD/JRC handbook](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2008/08/handbook-on-constructing-composite-indicators-methodology-and-user-guide_g1gh9301/9789264043466-en.pdf) [primary/institutional].

*(a) Gamed by (the fixes themselves):* EB shrinkage — keep n low to hide at the prior; Wilson bounds — volume inflation raises the bound; geometric-mean composites — a legitimate zero (no PRs) zeroes the person. *(b) Penalized:* low-volume excellent performers pulled toward the mean (show raw + shrunk + interval); non-PR engineers under any output-mandatory composite.

## 3. Real-implementation notes — what the code actually computes

Ten repositories were cloned (`--depth 1`) and read at named commits; three more deep-dived from a discovery sweep. All claims below are from reading the cited files [primary code]; steal/avoid judgments are **[inference]**.

### 3.1 Apache DevLake — [github.com/apache/incubator-devlake](https://github.com/apache/incubator-devlake) @ `15e34bb` (2026-07-02)

The most complete open implementation of DORA + AI-usage joins. Concrete computations: per-PR cycle decomposition in Go (`change_lead_time_calculator.go`): coding = first commit→PR open; pickup = PR open→first *non-author* review comment; review = first review→merge; deploy = merge→first successful production deployment whose **git-ancestry diff set** contains the merge SHA (attribution by commit graph, not timestamps). DORA dashboard SQL: deployment frequency = median over weeks of distinct deploy-days on a **zero-filled calendar spine**; CFR = incident-linked deployments/deployments where incidents are blamed on the *most recent* prior production deploy (explicitly temporal-heuristic); medians via PERCENT_RANK (lower-median). AI plugins ship per-user/per-day schemas: Copilot booleans (`used_agent/used_chat/used_cli`), Claude Code per-day sessions/commits/PR counts/accept-reject counts, Amazon Q credits. The AI⇄outcome joins are **org/team-week level only**: weekly `adoption_pct` vs cycle time with **Pearson r computed in SQL, returning NULL when n<4 weeks**; "Credits per Merged PR" exists — per ISO week, per org, never per person. Missing-data honesty: every DORA panel has an `_is_collected_data` CTE rendering "N/A. Please check if you have collected deployments/incidents" instead of a silent 0.

**Steal:** cycle decomposition; median-of-weekly-buckets on a calendar spine; benchmark-anchored coarse buckets instead of peer percentiles; min-n guards (r=NULL under 4 points); explicit N/A states (the direct fix for the CI-default-1.0 pathology); per-user tool-feature booleans as an adoption-depth vector; quality as a co-displayed axis, never a multiplier. **Avoid:** temporal incident-blame near people; exact-match identity with **no bot filtering in any metric SQL** (dependabot silently inflates counts); the `qdev_user_report` **named per-user spend table** (a privacy anti-pattern in the wild); credits-per-PR at individual grain — DevLake pointedly keeps it org-level.

### 3.2 Google Four Keys — [github.com/dora-team/fourkeys](https://github.com/dora-team/fourkeys) @ `6cc642e` (archived 2024)

Google's canonical DORA implementation. **Zero per-person attribution: no author/actor/user field appears in any metric query (grep-verified).** Everything is medians (`PERCENTILE_CONT 0.5`) bucketed into research-anchored labels (Daily/Weekly/…; CFR "0–15%"…), over rolling 3 months with `GENERATE_DATE_ARRAY` zero-filling. Change→failure linkage is bought with human conventions: an `Incident` issue label and a literal "root cause: `<sha>`" free-text regex — even Google couldn't get defect linkage without asking humans to annotate. Only automation defense: lead times ≤0 minutes nulled out. Found bug class: CFR's numerator/denominator granularity mismatch can push the "rate" above 1.0 **[inference from the SQL]**.

**Steal:** medians + coarse anchored buckets; zero-filled windows; rolling ~3-month horizon; nulling degenerate durations; the precedent itself — Google's reference implementation is team-only, success-filtered, bucket-based. **Avoid:** string-convention detection (CircleCI `LIKE "%deploy%"`) — it measures annotation discipline and is gamed by omission (exactly why the client's dropped "AI-made" tag must stay dead); silence on missing data (repos with no deploy events just vanish — the agent-platform engineers' problem in miniature); trusting author-controlled commit timestamps.

### 3.3 middlewarehq/middleware — [github.com/middlewarehq/middleware](https://github.com/middlewarehq/middleware) @ `a31c4da` (2026-06-09)

Team-level DORA (Flask/SQLAlchemy). Additive per-PR lead-time segments (commit→open→first-response→rework→merge→deploy) with named edge-case guards (negative first-commit-to-open clamped to 0; negative merge_time → NULL "Prevent garbage state"). `rework_cycles` = review→commit alternations between first blocking review and first approval. **Revert detection from branch names** (`revert-<pr#>`, GitHub's auto-revert convention) as a pure-metadata incident signal. Dual bot filtering on *events* (API `user.type=="Bot"` + name regex) — but bot-*authored* PRs still flow into metrics (a gap to not inherit). Composite "DORA score": per-pillar breakpoint ladders (0–10), missing pillars **dropped, not zeroed**, averaged, compared to hardcoded industry benchmarks of **uncited provenance**. In PR_MERGE fallback mode every merged PR counts as a deployment — a throughput metric silently degenerating into raw PR count. LOC stored per PR in `meta.code_stats` and **used in no metric** — deliberate display-only LOC.

**Steal:** additive time decomposition (the most interpretable pattern seen); revert-branch regex (top cheap acquisition); breakpoint ladders averaging only over pillars that exist; LOC-stored-never-scored. **Avoid:** PR-count-weighted arithmetic means everywhere; no-review PRs scoring as instant/elite (`merge_time=0` — less scrutiny must never look better); unmapped provider usernames as identity; benchmark constants without provenance.

### 3.4 Faros Community Edition — upstream deleted; analyzed via fork [JetsettingJames/faros-community-edition](https://github.com/JetsettingJames/faros-community-edition) @ `35e3a3a` (= final upstream `bc52033`, 2025-07-09)

The assigned repo **no longer exists** (GitHub 404, verified) — Faros pivoted closed-source. The AI Copilot dashboards (added Nov 2024) attribute by **person-period seat windows**: a PR is "with Tool" iff its author held a Copilot seat when the PR was created — cohort A/B instead of per-artifact attribution. Metrics: "PR Merge Rate" = distinct PRs / distinct authors (population-normalized), merge time, **median** linesAdded/Deleted. Acceptance rate computed by team/editor/language, never per person. Survey dashboard: per-PR-anchored time-saved question ("estimate how much total coding time you saved using copilot **for this PR**, in minutes") + weekly estimate ×5. **No dollar or token denominator anywhere; no composite score; three lenses (usage / impact / self-report) never collapsed.** Gaps: no bot filtering (`vcs_User.type` exists, never used), no cross-source identity resolution in CE, seat-window join can double-count across overlapping assignments, latest-build-status-only (rerun-until-green invisible), and a Top-5-Contributors PR leaderboard — the exact anti-pattern the brief forbids.

**Steal:** person-period attribution windows (our per-day usage data enables a *stronger* within-person version); per-PR-anchored self-report; the explicit `unaccounted_time` residual in its lead-time SQL (show what the model can't explain); tool-agnostic `{category, detail}` AI data model. **Avoid:** unlinked-count ratios (its CFR = incidents/deployments with no causal link — the same structural flaw as output-per-dollar); acceptance as impact; leaderboards.

### 3.5 copilot-metrics-viewer — [github.com/github-copilot-resources/copilot-metrics-viewer](https://github.com/github-copilot-resources/copilot-metrics-viewer) @ `9f42443` (2026-07-08)

GitHub's own dashboard over the current usage-metrics API. Per-user/day fields include the LoC funnel with **adds and deletes tracked separately** (`loc_suggested_to_add/delete_sum`, `loc_added/deleted_sum`), feature booleans (`used_agent/chat/cli`), CLI token usage, `ai_credits_used` (per-user spend, added 2026-06-19, kept `undefined` — **not 0** — when unreported), and GitHub's own opaque `ai_adoption_phase` cohort label with **unpublished criteria**. PR outcomes (incl. `total_created_by_copilot`, median-minutes-to-merge) exist **only at org level — GitHub does not attribute PR outcomes to individuals** [inference: the vendor does not consider it defensible]. Engineering discipline worth copying: roster zero-fill for adoption denominators; rolling 7/28-day distinct-user sets recomputed per day; shard dedup from atomic day records; **gross vs net spend** (fully-discounted plans report net=0 while gross reflects real usage); and a self-view privacy endpoint (`/api/my-usage`, non-admins see only their own row — the code comment calls the filter "the LOAD-BEARING security primitive").

**Steal:** funnel framing (suggested→accepted→retained); adoption-depth booleans; undefined≠zero; gross-not-net dollars for any spend display; the self-view privacy architecture. **Avoid:** acceptance rate as quality (GitHub redefined the counters across API versions — "activities, not individual suggestions" — silently changing every downstream rate); opaque vendor cohort labels as score inputs; single-vendor telemetry as the spend universe.

### 3.6 CHAOSS — definitions in [chaoss/wg-metrics-development](https://github.com/chaoss/wg-metrics-development) + [chaoss/wg-evolution](https://github.com/chaoss/wg-evolution); implementation [chaoss/CollectOSS](https://github.com/chaoss/CollectOSS) @ `adfa6e8` (assigned `chaoss/knowledge-base` **does not exist**; estate mapped)

The Linux Foundation's metrics body. Structure worth copying wholesale: **Goal-Question-Metric** — every metric file opens with a single written `Question:`, carries named parameters with defaults ("Date type: author date or committer date. Default: author date"), and a standing privacy/ethics warning. **No released CHAOSS metric is a per-person score** — contributor is a *filter*, never the unit of analysis; every CollectOSS metric endpoint is keyed `(repo_group_id, repo_id, period)`. Practitioner-guide verbatim: "Be very careful with metrics that can be used to compare people against each other in ways that might result in the punishment of individuals"; "never set yourself up for people to weaponize your metrics" ([chaoss.community](https://chaoss.community/practitioner-guide-introduction/) [primary]). Implementation details: the Facade commit analyzer zeroes merge commits, reclassifies whitespace-only and re-indent lines, detects renames — a concrete "meaningful lines" floor; SortingHat models **`is_bot` as a property of the resolved identity**, set once, inherited by all metrics; bots are a first-class *measured* category (Bot Activity metric) rather than discarded; where trace data can't attribute (their Contribution Attribution ≈ our AI-attribution gap), CHAOSS's answer is **structured self-report**, not inference; contributor classes use tunable cadence filters, not eligibility cliffs.

**Steal:** GQM discipline (each dashboard number answers one written question — the cheapest interpretability win); published parameter defaults; identity-layer bot flagging; attribute automation back to the owner as a visible lane; cadence classes over cliffs; the three ethics tests before shipping any subscore. **Avoid:** per-person health scores (the field's most experienced measurers refused to build the client's current metric); acceptance-ratio-style quality (their CR Acceptance Ratio saturates exactly like CI-clean); timestamp-pattern metrics (privacy hazard CHAOSS itself flags).

### 3.7 code-maat — [github.com/adamtornhill/code-maat](https://github.com/adamtornhill/code-maat) @ `50537ab` (2025-07-03)

Tornhill's book-companion analyzer (evolved into CodeScene). Per-author formulas: author-churn (Σ added/deleted/commits); ownership = main-dev share of an entity's added lines; **refactoring-main-dev** = argmax of *deleted* lines ("when you remove code, you make a more active design choice"); fragmentation = 1 − Herfindahl concentration of revisions. README verbatim, on the tool's own per-author churn: **"you wouldn't use this data for any performance evaluation… (in case anything should be rewarded it would be a net deletion of code - there's too much of it in the world)."** Design mechanics: a team-mapping pre-stage remaps authors *before* analysis (same algorithms, coarser identity); a sliding-window regrouper treats commits within N days as one logical change to neutralize commit-style bias; change sets >30 files are **dropped, not capped**; coupling metrics gate rows on min-revs thresholds.

**Steal:** identity-mapping pre-stage; logical-change windowing (neutralizes PR/commit slicing before counting); drop-don't-winsorize for pathological change sets; concentration indices for distributional subscores; vendored/generated path exclusion before any line counting. **Avoid:** LOC-as-contribution (the tool ships it and its author tells you not to use it on people); fixed inclusion thresholds (min-revs=5 ≈ the client's brittle ≥3-PR floor); raw author-string identity.

### 3.8 git-of-theseus — [github.com/erikbern/git-of-theseus](https://github.com/erikbern/git-of-theseus) @ `961bda0` (2023-11-25)

Line-survival archaeology: weekly first-parent snapshots, incremental blame, Kaplan-Meier-like aggregate survival curves, exponential half-life fits (Linux 6.60y, Rails 2.43y, aggregate ≈3.33y — [erikbern.com](https://erikbern.com/2016/12/05/the-half-life-of-code.html) [practitioner]). Why per-engineer half-life fails as a leverage proxy: gamed by writing untouched cold code, blame-capture via mass reformat sweeps, ownership gatekeeping; penalizes fast-iterating product teams (repo choice spans a **~20x** half-life spread that dwarfs any person effect), refactorers, new hires (right-censored); feedback latency is *years* against a 4–12-week window; and it requires blaming source trees. The tool's two disciplines transfer anyway: **age-relative alignment** (compare work at the same age since merge) and **right-censoring** (commits too recent to have died leave the risk set — never scored as survivors), the statistical inverse of the client's CI-defaults-to-1.0.

### 3.9 Hercules — [github.com/src-d/hercules](https://github.com/src-d/hercules) @ `68bb211` (2022-11-29; dormant)

Industrial-strength burndown: interval-tree incremental blame, per-developer line-survival matrices, a proper **lifelines Kaplan-Meier fit with right-censoring**, an Added/Removed/**Changed** hunk decomposition (paired delete-insert = "changed" — a cheap GitClear-lite rework taxonomy), and an overwrites matrix that **separates self-overwrites from being-overwritten-by-others** (iteration vs durability — exactly the distinction a churn signal needs). ### 3.10 satomic/copilot-usage-advanced-dashboard, PR-analytics, ai-detector (discovery sweep)

### 3.11 Cross-cutting implementation lessons

1. **No OSS project joins per-user AI usage with delivery output.** Adoption dashboards stop at usage; delivery dashboards stop at PRs. The client's "leverage" join has no reference implementation anywhere — this design is genuinely un-copied **[inference]**.
2. **Nobody defends against bots by default** — fourkeys (none), Faros CE (`type` field unused), middleware (events only, not authors), DevLake (none in metric SQL). Any per-person score must add identity resolution + bot/service-account filtering as step zero.
3. **The strongest tools refuse individual scoring on purpose** — fourkeys, CHAOSS, Athenian, Uplevel, code-maat's README, DevLake's org-level joins. The self-view-only design is *more* individual than anything the field ships; that is only defensible with the guardrails in §6.
4. **Missing data must be a labeled state** — DevLake's N/A CTEs and copilot-viewer's undefined≠0 versus fourkeys' silent vanishing and the client's fake-neutral CI 1.0.
5. **Medians + coarse anchored buckets + zero-filled spines** are the recurring robustness kit; live peer percentiles appear nowhere in the credible implementations.

## 4. Critique of the current metric — point by point

The metric under critique (brief, verbatim mechanics): `score = mean( pctile_within_level(PRs_merged × CI_clean / spend$), pctile_within_level(Σ min(lines_per_PR, 2000) × CI_clean / spend$) )`, 4-week window, CI_clean defaulting to 1.0 under 3 checked PRs, AND-ed floors (≥3 merged PRs, ≥$5, ≥3 active days, ≥14 days), cohorts = job level only (FT/contractor/intern buckets).

**Verdict up front (construct validity):** this is not a noisy measure of leverage — it is a valid-looking measure of a different construct, **"metered-spend frugality × PR-shaped volume,"** anti-correlated with the most defensible definitions of leverage. It fails all four classical validity tests (content, convergent, discriminant, criterion), and its optimum is reached by minimizing AI use. Not fixable by re-weighting; the construct must be replaced (full scorecard in `crit-construct.md`).

### 4.1 The seven known problems, confirmed and sharpened

**P1 — CI-clean-rate is a weak quality proxy (confirmed; it is statistically dead).** Two mechanisms pile mass at exactly 1.0: AI iterates to green before push (causal Goodhart — Cui: build *volume* +38.4% while success *rate* was flat-to-negative, −17.4% significant at Accenture [primary]), and the fake-neutral default under 3 checked PRs. A variable ≈1.0 for most of the population carries near-zero information; multiplying by it changes nothing — so "quality-adjusted" is nominal, and the 25–45% security-flaw rates in CI-passing AI code (§2.7) are the omitted, AI-correlated variance. **No transform rescues it**: shrinkage on a saturated signal converges all posteriors to the prior.

*Worked example — the lottery penalty and evidence inversion* **[inference — illustration]**: binomial with true per-PR clean probability p=0.9, n=3 checked PRs: P(3/3)=0.729 → 1.00; P(2/3)=0.243 → 0.667 (**−33% on BOTH signals**); P(≤1/3)=0.028 → ≤0.333. A genuinely good engineer eats a ≥33% score haircut **27.1% of windows** from a single flaky test — while a colleague with 2 checked PRs is pinned at 1.0, *above any honest estimate of anyone's rate*. Fewer data ⇒ better score: the default inverts the evidence ordering.

**P2 — Narrow spend denominator; agent-platform work invisible (confirmed; the error is categorical, not marginal).** The denominator meters a CLI allowlist; identical work through chat tools, personal accounts, or the org's own agent platform moves the measured denominator with zero change in behavior. Agent-platform engineers have spend and no PR-shaped output: they are either excluded by the ≥3-PR floor or occupy the bottom percentiles **by construction**. No published methodology anywhere scores an individual by the agents they build (§2.10 — honest dead end); DORA's platform J-curve is direct evidence delivery metrics mis-price platform work in flight. The metric doesn't under-rate this population — it is *categorically wrong* about them, and (worse) its gradient tells the org's most AI-native builders to stop building agents and go farm PRs.

**P3 — Right-skewed per-dollar distribution, top scorers decided by noise (confirmed, quantified).**
- *Ranking inversion is empirical, not hypothetical:* on Jellyfish's published quintiles, the top-quintile engineer produces **2.1× the merged PRs** but scores **~290× lower** on PRs-per-dollar ($0.28 vs $89.32/PR) [vendor]. Percentile-ranked, the demonstrably most productive engineers land at the bottom of a "leverage" score.
- *Denominator-dominated variance:* delta method, CV²(X/Y) ≈ CV²(X)+CV²(Y)−2ρ·CV(X)CV(Y). With 4-week PR count CV=0.7 (consistent with Cui's published moments), spend CV=1.0 (conservative given $3–$1,822 quintile means), ρ=0.3 → **CV(ratio) ≈ 103%** **[inference — illustration]**. One heavy agent session moves the ratio by tens of percent with zero behavior change.
- *Percentile noise amplification:* modeling the per-dollar value log-normal (σ=1), a 1-SD noise fluctuation near the median moves the displayed score **±34 percentile points**, while a genuine 28% improvement moves ~10 — the display amplifies noise for the mid-cohort majority and compresses real differences in the tail, backwards on both ends **[inference — illustration]**. This is why "top scorers often just have marginally better CI rate": selecting the top of a noisy proxy selects the noise (regressional Goodhart).

**P4 — Brittle cold-start floors (confirmed, quantified).** Four AND-ed gates make score *existence* a step function of zero-inflated counts. Modeling a typical engineer's 4-week merged-PR count as Poisson(3.5) **[inference — illustration]**: P(N≤2) ≈ **0.32** — a 1-in-3 chance of simply having no score in any month; and E[N | N≥3] ≈ 4.45 vs true mean 3.5 — the visible months carry a **+27% upward bias**, so the engineer's own trend line systematically shows lucky months then "regresses." The $5 gate also parks scored engineers *next to the ratio's singularity*: 3 PRs at $5.01 = 0.599 PRs/$ outranks 30 PRs at $500 = 0.06 PRs/$ by 10×.

**P5 — "Output per dollar" penalizes spending more on harder work (confirmed; and spend is not even a difficulty signal).** Identical tasks vary up to **30x** in token cost and cost only weakly tracks expert-rated difficulty ([arXiv 2604.22750](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22750) [independent]) — so a per-dollar rank punishes hard work *and* can't be repaired by difficulty adjustment (spend proxies neither difficulty nor waste reliably). Two denominator families must be distinguished: **exposure normalizers** (per active day/week — legitimate; correct for opportunity) vs **investment denominators** (dollars/tokens — structurally punish investment; extremal Goodhart; the honest summary of spend-vs-output is a *curve position*, not a scalar). A per-dollar scalar as a personal headline is unsalvageable.

**P6 — Level-only cross-function cohorts (confirmed; the cohort controls the weakest axis and ignores the dominant ones).** Function/repo environment drive far larger structural spreads than anything behavioral: 3–5x achievable-gain differences by seniority/context (Cui; Stanford gradient), ~300x cost-per-PR spread (Jellyfish), ~20x repo half-life spread — against ~1.26x for the largest causal behavioral effect (Cui pooled). The percentile is substantially **a function classifier wearing a leverage costume** **[inference]**. The naive fix is also wrong: hard function×level splits give ~36 cells over 500–5,000 engineers; percentile resolution is 100/n, so a 12-person cell moves in ~8-point jumps — the correct tool is hierarchical partial pooling. And percentiles are zero-sum + non-stationary during an adoption wave: your score falls when colleagues adopt faster, with no behavior change — uninterpretable and demotivating for a self-view product.

**P7 — CI-clean-rate barely discriminates and penalizes heavy-CI repos (confirmed; the unfairness is arithmetic).** A PR is clean only if every job passes: with per-job pass probability 0.995 **[inference — illustration]**, a 5-job repo expects 0.995⁵ ≈ **0.975** clean rate; a 50-job repo 0.995⁵⁰ ≈ **0.778** — a ~20-point gap from repo configuration alone, identical engineer. Because saturation pushes everyone else to 1.0, the off-ceiling minority the multiplier actually discriminates among is *disproportionately composed of heavy/flaky-CI residents* — the adjustment penalizes the repo, not the person. Grain mismatch on top: CI_clean is computed over checked PRs but multiplies *all* merged PRs, so unchecked PRs inherit the checked ones' quality credit.

### 4.2 New problems the critiques found (beyond the known seven)

**N1 — The two "signals" are one signal, and that signal is mostly not-spending.** In logs, both signals share the CI term and the spend term; only correlated volume numerators differ. Estimating spend dispersion from Jellyfish's quintile means gives σ_log-spend ≈ 2.3 **[inference — derivation, assumes log-normality]**; with plausible σ_log-PRs=0.5, σ_log-LOC=0.9, σ_log-CI≈0: **~95% of each signal's variance is the −log(spend) term, and corr(log s₁, log s₂) ≈ 0.91**. Averaging two ~0.9-correlated signals adds no information — pseudo-triangulation — and the composite is, to first order, **a percentile rank of not spending**: the untested mirror image of the failed Meta/Amazon token leaderboards, inheriting their Goodhart dynamics with the sign flipped.

**N2 — The assembly commits two documented statistical anti-patterns in one line.** Percentile ranks are ordinal and not averageable [reference]; the arithmetic mean of normalized ratios yields normalization-dependent, inconsistent rankings — geometric mean is the invariant summary (Fleming & Wallace, CACM 1986 [primary]).

**N3 — The 2000-line winsorization cap is itself the gaming instruction.** A 6,000-line change as one PR scores (1 PR, 2,000 capped lines); as 3×2,000-line PRs it scores (**3 PRs, 6,000 lines**) — splitting under the published cap **triples both numerators simultaneously**, tells the engineer the exactly optimal PR size, and the effective winsorization depends on PR granularity (the estimator is inconsistent across work styles). The fixed constant also binds differentially by function (data-eng migrations vs frontend), counts deletions as additions (Atkinson's −2000-line improvement scores like 2,000 lines of padding), and standard practice caps at distribution percentiles chosen by sensitivity analysis, not absolute constants.

**N4 — The 4-week window cannot support per-person inference at all.** Cui needed n≈5,000 engineers over months for PR effects to be barely significant. Reliability sketch **[inference — illustration]**: with within-person noise σ_log ≈ 0.83 (from N/P3's CV≈100%) and a generous true between-person σ_log of 0.5–0.7, test-retest reliability ≈ **0.27–0.42** — the majority of month-over-month movement is sampling noise before any gaming. Engineers will attribute noise to their behavior (METR shows exactly how badly AI self-perception calibrates).

**N5 — Ambient gaming: the score inflates with nobody cheating.** The metric's inputs drift in the gamer's direction as AI workflows mature: CI-clean → 1.0 (iterate-to-green is the product design of coding agents); PR counts and sizes inflate organically (+98%/+154%, Faros); churn/copy-paste rise (GitClear) — while system outcomes can move the other way (DORA 2024). Month-over-month gains read as "my leverage improved" while measuring workflow drift: **the metric cannot distinguish a better engineer from a later calendar date**, which also breaks any naive self-vs-self trend built on the same inputs.

### 4.3 The rational gamer's playbook (adversarial critique, condensed)

Attack leverage is asymmetric — the composite is effectively one lever: cut measured spend $200→$5.01 = both ratios **×~40**; split 5 PRs into 15 = ×3; lift CI 0.90→1.00 = ×1.11. A rational gamer attacks the denominator first, and the denominator is the least-defended input.

| # | Exploit | Mechanics | Effort / detectability (HAVE data) | Who it beats |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Iterate-to-green CI saturation** | Zero intent needed — it *is* the AI workflow; population already at ~1.0 | None / n.a. — the signal is dead | Heavy-CI/flaky-repo engineers; risk-takers; CI-as-iteration users |
| 2 | **Spend minimization** (route via chat/agent platform/personal accounts; $5.01 thrift; cheap-model theater) | ~40x mathematical lever; policy-compliant; mirror of the failed Meta/Amazon leaderboards | None–Low / Medium but unactionable (mix-shift ≙ legitimate preference) | Heavy legitimate adopters (2x-productive per Jellyfish); learners; compliant meterees |
| 3 | **PR splitting / micro-PR inflation** | Quadruple-dips: PR count ×k, lines under the cap, easier CI, clears the ≥3-PR floor; cloaked as DORA best practice | Low / Low-Med (pile-up under 2,000 lines is the telltale) | Big-feature/ML engineers; reviewers (cost-shift: +91% review time, Faros) |
| 4 | **Effort reallocation to PR-shaped work** (the Austin attack) | Shift time from review/mentoring/incidents/agent-building into merged PRs — not cheating, *obeying the dashboard* | None / very Low | Mentors, reviewers, incident responders, agent builders — the org's highest-leverage people |
| 5 | **Line farming + churn recycling** | Verbose generation, lockfile/vendored churn, merge-then-rewrite (every pass counts as fresh lines; churn up to 9x for heavy AI users) | None / near-zero (counts only, no file/content data) | Simplifiers/deleters, right-first-time seniors, terse-language stacks |

Also cataloged: the default-1.0 exploit (≤2 checked PRs = the *maximum* multiplier while non-CI PRs count fully); revert/rework double-dip (ship broken → revert → fix = 3 merged PRs and ~3× lines from one failed unit — the metric literally pays extra for failure); score suppression (deliberately fail one floor to convert a bad month into "no score"); merge-timing/seasonal arbitrage against the zero-sum percentile; codemod fan-out (one AI codemod → 30–50 personally-authored PRs). The **combined play** — spend routing + stacked micro-PRs + non-CI paths + cohort-lull merges — yields a top-of-cohort score with *negative* org value, every component individually plausible and policy-compliant. What survives the red team: self-view-only privacy (the strongest control), the non-manipulability of cohort assignment, and exactly two emitted telltales (PR-size pile-up under the cap; falling CI-checked share) — kept as integrity canaries in the successor design.

### 4.4 Fairness verdict matrix (who the metric wrongs, and whether cohorts can fix it)

| Axis | Mechanism | Rough magnitude | Cohort redesign enough? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Function | PR/line/spend norms + achievable gains are function properties | 3–5x gains spread; ~300x cost/PR spread vs ~1.26x behavioral effects | Partial — pooling fixes the comparison; ratio + volume numerators stay broken |

## 5. Candidate approaches — ranked

Five full design candidates were developed (each honoring the critiques' hard constraints: no spend denominator, no CI multiplier, no percentile averaging, no fake-neutral defaults, no hard AND-gate floors, no level-only cohorts), then judged and red-teamed. Ranking reflects the adversarial weighting the mission mandated (gaming-resistance and fairness weighted first, then interpretability, cold-start behavior, and data-readiness).

| Rank | Name | Construct | Gaming-resistance | Fairness | Interpretability | Cold-start | Data-readiness |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **AI Leverage Profile (ALP, Anchored Hybrid)** — recommended | Practice + trajectory: 5 non-compensatory facets, categorical self-vs-self headline | **High** post red-team — top state needs externally-verified output; bounded theater residual | **High** — profiles, FTE-scaled anchors, unmeasured states, per-repo normalization | High — every clause calendar-verifiable; no hidden weights | Graceful — provisional/building states, no cliffs | v0 on HAVE + 2 trivial config tables; v1 needs 3 cheap acquisitions |
| 2 | **Practice Maturity Ladder (PML)** | Usage-pattern maturity (0–4 rule ladder) + quarterly cohort validation loop | Medium — scored construct counterfeitable by a scripted session habit; payoffs bounded | High — no output demanded; agent-platform first-class | **Highest** — pure counts, published thresholds | Best — computable from week 1 | Highest — HAVE + config only |
| 3 | **Trajectory (self-relative trend + uplift)** | Direction of own output/practice trend, cohort-drift-corrected; quarantined uplift contrast | Medium-High — sandbagging decays; but headline underpowered | High — self-vs-self; regime resets | Medium — slopes with CIs need statistical literacy | Slow — 8-week baselines; interns never scored | HAVE-only |
| 4 | **Net Delivery Goodput (NDG)** | Merged volume net of reverts/self-rework/overhead, per exposure week, banded | Medium — v1 solid; **v0 is log-tempered LOC (inflatable)** | Medium — deletions full weight, per-cell norms; still PR-shaped | High — "durable lines/week" + 5 frozen bands | Good — EB shrinkage, no floors | v1 requires file lists + revert linkage + add/del split |

**5.1 ALP — AI Leverage Profile (rank 1).** *Formula:* five facets — Practice (rule ladder over meaningful-use days), Flow (EB-shrunk share of PRs inside repo-relative size bands), Quality floor (per-repo anomaly flags, cap-only), Momentum (Theil–Sen self-vs-self trend driving a categorical headline), Experience (quarterly friction-facts pulse) — plus an unscored cost strip; nothing averaged. Full spec in §6. *HAVE vs NEW:* v0 ships on HAVE + a tool-class config table and seat/licensing table; the quality floor's substance (path exclusion, revert linkage, re-touch churn) and the agent-output facet are NEW acquisitions. *Gaming:* the old top-five exploits are dead (no denominator, no line numerator, no CI multiplier, no live percentiles, no floors); residuals are bounded practice theater (private label, canaried) and cosmetic PR splitting in Flow (declared unfixable without diff content). *Fairness:* function×level precision-weighted cohorts for context only; work-mode profiles; FTE-scaled anchors; explicit unmeasured states for review/mentoring/agent work. *Interpretability:* every clause is a count the engineer can check against their calendar; the headline is a sentence with its evidence attached. *Cold start:* provisional stages from week 1, "Building baseline (k of 9)," 24-week new-hire protection from negative labels; no on/off flicker.

**5.2 PML — Practice Maturity Ladder (rank 2; its best organs are grafted into ALP).** *Formula:* meaningful-use day = sessions ≥1 AND tokens ≥ τ_c (published p10 floor); components C1 consistency, C2 breadth/licensed classes, C3 agentic establishment, C4 recency → breakpoint ladder Stage 0–4; quarterly within-person validation loop that demotes any rung whose sustained transitions don't precede better delivery trends. *HAVE vs NEW:* fully shippable on HAVE + two trivial config tables; all NEW items upgrade validation, not the score. *Gaming:* consumption is structurally unrewarded (tokens above τ_c and all dollars contribute nothing) — but the whole scored construct is usage-side telemetry: a scripted session habit counterfeits it end-to-end (red-team confirmed the spoof is one crontab), which is why it lost as the *sole* design despite the highest raw judge total. *Fairness:* its best case — agent-platform engineers become first-class (their sessions/days/runs are score inputs); its residual risk is Stage 4 hard-coding "agentic > chat" for roles where that is unsettled, and batch deep-workers whose rhythm is C1-hostile. *Interpretability:* the strongest of all five — "you used AI meaningfully in 10 of 12 working weeks, across 2 of 3 tool classes." *Cold start:* components computable from week 1; INSUFFICIENT OBSERVATION is a labeled state.

**5.3 Trajectory (rank 3; its regime-reset machinery is grafted into ALP).** *Formula:* T = Theil–Sen slope of own log weekly merged output minus cohort median trend (drift correction); U = own AI-heavy-tercile weeks vs AI-light weeks (median contrast, permutation-banded, labeled "association, not causation"); P = practice-depth rubric; quality guardrail as flags; no composite. *HAVE vs NEW:* HAVE-only; churn/revert and caller-identity acquisitions upgrade the guardrail and the platform profile. *Gaming:* baseline sandbagging decays (rolling baseline) and buys one transient window; ambient-drift masquerade is countered by cohort-trend subtraction — but the red team later showed live detrending re-opens a victim/cartel channel and Theil–Sen tie-degeneracy fabricates "Drifting" for sparse engineers (both fixed in ALP's revision). *Fairness:* seniors' "Stable" is a first-class good state; agent-platform engineers get an explicit "output unmeasurable" state. *Interpretability:* honest but demanding — most engineers will read "no detectable trend" most quarters. *Cold start:* the slowest — U needs 26 weeks of history; interns honestly never get trends.

**5.4 NDG — Net Delivery Goodput (rank 4; its netting discipline is grafted into ALP's quality floor).** *Formula:* net_lines = effective lines (add+del, full deletion weight, excluded paths) − reverted lines (both legs zeroed) − *excess* self-rework ≤21d (only beyond the cell-median allowance, per Nagappan & Ball's relative-churn result); weekly, week-level winsorized at cell P95 (splitting-neutral); log1p per exposure week; hierarchical z; 5 fixed annually-anchored bands + self-trend. *HAVE vs NEW:* the design is only defensible at v1 — per-PR file lists, revert linkage, and add/del splits are prerequisites; its own text concedes v0 "should lose to a practice/trend design." *Gaming:* splitting-neutral by construction; verbose real-path code survives until rewritten (largest declared surface, partially self-limiting because padding attracts rework charged back to its author). *Fairness:* deletions at full weight rewards simplification; still PR-shaped — review/mentoring/agent work unmeasured (declared). *Interpretability:* "durable lines/week" is a strong plain-language unit. *Cold start:* provisional bands from week 1 with intervals.

**5.5 LM-E — AI Leverage Map (rank 5; the steelman of the client's cost framing; only its showback strip survives into ALP).** *Formula:* X = log spend per active day (all tools), banded on frozen cohort quartiles; Y = EB-shrunk weekly merged-PR median, banded; quadrant labels protective by design (high-X/low-Y = "Investment phase," never "wasteful"); headline = within-person Theil–Sen elasticity of log-output on log-spend across own weeks, with bootstrap CI and an "insufficient variation" state for uniformly-heavy spenders. *HAVE vs NEW:* HAVE-only. *Gaming:* spend suppression buys nothing (an axis, not a divisor); elasticity fake-able by coordinating spend and merge timing across ≥8 weeks (expensive, private payoff). *Fairness:* monorepo/large-context engineers read "Heavy" for identical work (input tokens dominate cost) — mitigated by band coarseness and zero valence, residual acknowledged. *Interpretability:* the map is legible; the elasticity mostly is not — CIs straddle zero for most engineers (Cui needed n≈5,000 for significance), so the headline defaults to ◆ flat/uncertain. *Cold start:* good (states, EB intervals). *Why it lost:* it promotes the weakest-validity quantity in the whole design space — within-person spend-output covariation — to the headline; the judge panel unanimously deleted every spend-elasticity/uplift estimator from the recommended product.

## 6. Recommended design — the AI Leverage Profile (ALP, Anchored Hybrid)

This is the committed design: the ALP chassis (five non-compensatory facets, no composite, unscored cost strip) with four grafts — PML's published rule anchors + validation loop, NDG's durability netting (as flags only), Trajectory's regime resets — and one deletion (all spend-elasticity/uplift estimators), **revised after three red-team passes** (10 gaming, 20 fairness, 14 statistics attacks; every disposition logged in §6.5). No MCMC, no live posteriors: everything is closed-form (rule ladders, frozen blended quantile snapshots, method-of-moments Beta-binomial EB with degenerate-case fallback, Theil–Sen + block bootstrap) and implementable as SQL + a small batch job.

**Construct, named honestly:** "leverage" is *not* presented as a measured amplification factor — the per-person counterfactual is unmeasurable under the hard limits (METR: −19% actual vs +20% believed [independent]; the 2026 update calls its own reversal "only very weak evidence"). ALP operationalizes leverage as **practice + trajectory**: how closely the engineer's observable usage pattern matches practices evidence associates with durable, system-friendly output (DORA capability framing [primary]), and the direction of their own practice and output trend **vs their own baseline**, with cohort drift shown as unscored context. Quality can only cap, never boost.

### 6.1 Global machinery (every parameter, with its one-line justification)

| Parameter | Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Facet window | rolling 12 weeks, weekly buckets, zero-filled calendar spine | 4-week reliability ≈0.3 (§4, N4); brief-sanctioned; matches DX Core 4 lookback [vendor] |
| Momentum history | trailing 26 weeks, **biweekly buckets (13 points)** | 12 weekly points have near-zero trend power (red-team S1: a 90% CI excludes zero only at ≥4.2× growth/quarter) |
| AS_OF | **per-source completeness watermarks** (latest day with row count ≥99% of trailing-4-week average); sub-watermark weeks = "data pending", excluded from numerators AND denominators | "~2 weeks" lag is variable; a constant silently demotes stages via pipeline latency (S10) |
| Refresh | weekly recompute; UI states loop latency plainly ("changes reach facets in ~3 weeks, the trajectory in ~2 months") | honesty about the feedback loop (S10) |
| Cohort c(e) | function × level (≈6×6); small cells use a **precision-weighted blend** w = n/(n+20) toward the function level — no hard switch | function/repo dominate variance (Cui; Jellyfish); a hard n<20 fallback is a relocated cliff (S13) |
| Cohort-derived constants (τ_c, repo bands, cutpoints) | computed on trailing history, **frozen per period, refreshed as a blend** (0.75·old + 0.25·new, quarterly; τ_c monthly), published with changelog and pre-announced migrations | yearly hard freezes lock small-cell noise in, then fire a synchronized mass-demotion cliff at refresh (S5) |

Profiles change **display emphasis only — every facet computes whenever its own data floor is met**; changes persist ≥8 weeks; a switch never retires an active flag | the original router had a hole (low-PR chat-heavy engineers fell into full pr-shaped scoring, F3), an undefined basis (tokens misroute on 30x cost variance), and a laundering dodge (A1) |

### 6.2 The five facets and the headline

**Facet P — Practice (rule-anchored ladder 0–4; all profiles).**
```
class(t) ∈ {CHAT, CODING_AGENT, AGENT_PLATFORM}    -- config table, named owner, published criteria
τ_c = P10 of the nonzero per-DAY token distribution per class, org-wide trailing 26w, monthly blended
      -- grain matters: the first spec tested a DAILY total against a per-SESSION P10 → no-op floor (S7)
      -- τ_c is honestly labeled a data-hygiene floor, NOT an anti-theater defense
meaningful(u,d,c) = sessions ≥ 1 AND tokens(u,d,c) ≥ τ_c
      -- AGENT_PLATFORM: the runs≥1 requirement is dropped in v0 (run owner-vs-caller attribution is
      -- unresolved; either resolution is unfair or gameable, F7); runs feed only a canary
active_week(u,w) = any AUTHORED PR event OR any AI session   -- review-only weeks are invisible v0;
      -- post-acquisition #5 they become "present — work not measurable here" (counts toward AW,
      -- excluded from C1's denominator)

C1 CONSISTENCY = mean over active weeks of max( min(days_w,3)/3 , min(days_trailing4w,6)/6 )   -- graded:
      -- the binary ≥2-day week test inverted the construct (a perfectly consistent 1-day/week user
      -- scored below a lumpy 2-day user) and made one light day worse than abstention (S6/A8)
C2 BREADTH     = Σ_c min(1, meaningful_days(c)/⌈5·FTE⌉) / n_classes_available(u)
      -- availability counts a class only from the user's first seat-assignment date (F19)
C3 AGENTIC     = min(1, meaningful_days(CODING_AGENT ∪ AGENT_PLATFORM)/⌈10·FTE⌉)
C4 RECENCY     = share of the last 4 ACTIVE weeks with ≥1 meaningful day
      -- on active weeks, not calendar weeks: the calendar form demoted Stage 3 after a 2–3 week
      -- vacation, below the ≥4-week reset trigger (F14)

AW < 6 → "LOW OBSERVABLE ACTIVITY" ("much of your work may not be visible to us");
         the provisional stage is ALWAYS shown with a wide-uncertainty badge — never a blank
Stage 0 EXPLORING:  C1 < 0.25          Stage 1 OCCASIONAL: C1 ≥ 0.25
Stage 2 REGULAR:    C1 ≥ 0.50 AND ≥1 established class
Stage 3 INTEGRATED: C1 ≥ 0.75 AND established_classes ≥ min(2, n_classes_available) AND C4 ≥ 0.5
      -- min(2,n): the ratio form made Stage 3 mathematically UNREACHABLE for single-class-licensed
      -- teams — the exact population the denominator was built to protect (F1)
Stage 4 FRONTIER:   Stage 3 AND C3 = 1 AND ≥1 externally-verified event in the window
      -- an authored MERGED PR in a week with agentic-class activity, or (post-acq #1) ≥1 distinct
      -- NON-SELF caller of an agent they own. The old "no canary flag" condition is DELETED — it
      -- contradicted "canaries are never per-person" (A2/F20). Top rung is no longer reachable from
      -- purely self-generated telemetry. Stage 3 is the published TARGET; Stage 4 explicitly optional
      -- (the evidence has not settled agentic>chat for all roles — METR seniors tension).
```
Thresholds (0.25/0.50/0.75, 5d, 10d, τ_c=P10) are judgment calls dressed as constants: published, with a quarterly ±30% sensitivity analysis reporting the % of engineers whose stage would change (OECD/JRC practice [primary/institutional]).

**Facet F — Flow (batch-size discipline; computed whenever the PR data floor is met).**
```
lines_changed := additions + deletions (defined; deletions equal weight — pro-refactorer)
in_band(pr) := 10 ≤ lines_changed ≤ P75(lines per PR | band_grain, trailing 26w, frozen-blended qtrly)
   band_grain = repo IF the repo has ≥30 PRs in 26w (min-evidence: a solo author can no longer
   self-set their band by landing huge PRs pre-freeze, A7); else function-level band; shared
   monorepos above a volume threshold band by repo × author-function-cell (F11)
F posterior = Beta(in_band + α_f, PRs − in_band + β_f), (α_f,β_f) per function by method-of-moments
   with a weakly-informative fallback (strength 10 at the pooled mean) when the fit degenerates (S12)
Banding: assign band b iff P(true share > cutpoint_b | data) > 0.6 — POSTERIOR-PROBABILITY banding
   (point-estimate banding was volume-confounded: a 4-PR perfect record could never reach the top
   band while a 40-PR one coasted, S9). Raw share and n always displayed ("9 of 15 in band").
```
Small batches are DORA's causal story for AI-era instability; F **inverts** the old lines numerator — size stops being rewarded, discipline is. F is a share: more PRs never raise it. Slice-at-merge-time cosmetic splitting is F's declared residual — indistinguishable from genuine small-batch practice without diff content.

**Facet Q — Quality floor (a state, never a score; can only CAP the headline).**
```
Scope: STRICTLY PER REPO — clean/checked never pool across repos (pooling reintroduces repo-mix
   bias, F10). Computable only at ≥10 checked PRs in the repo ("not computable" below — never "clear").
v0 flag_CI in repo r iff Wilson95_upper(clean_r, checked_r) < max(P10(rate_EB | repo tier),
                                                                  median(rate_EB | repo tier) − 0.05)
   -- reference CHANGED from the median: a below-median test flagged a true-0.9 engineer 27% of
   -- windows at n=3 and converges to flagging ~half of high-volume engineers (S3). The new
   -- reference is a true outlier boundary with a declared non-inferiority margin.
   rate_EB = (clean + α_r)/(checked + α_r + β_r) per repo (blend to function), MoM + fallback.
   Evaluated at MONTHLY FROZEN boundaries (weekly sliding tests made "persistence" free, S3);
   entering `attention` needs 2 consecutive independent monthly evaluations; EXIT is immediate;
   flags auto-expire; a self-annotation ("incident remediation") displays beside the flag (F12).
v1 (3 cheap acquisitions) adds flags: flag_revert (both-legs revert zeroing, KEYED TO THE REVERTED
   PR'S AUTHOR — else reciprocal-revert pairs launder both parties, A5); flag_churn (own-file
   re-touch ≤21d beyond repo-median allowance — only EXCESS churn, relative not absolute, per
   Nagappan & Ball [independent]); flag_review (merged-without-review above repo norm, EXEMPTING
   incident/deploy-labeled break-glass merges, F15).
Q_state ∈ {clear (n=…), attention:[flags], not computable} — evidence always displayed with the
   state: "Clear (4 checked PRs)" ≠ "Clear (34 checked PRs)" (A5).
```
No default exists below the floor — "not computable", never clean. This kills the fake-1.0 pathology *and* its epistemic twin, the low-evidence "Clear."

**Facet M — Momentum (RAW self-vs-self; cohort drift as context) — the headline driver.**
```
Series: biweekly buckets over trailing 26w (13 points); require ≥9 usable buckets; data-pending and
   self-marked on-call/incident buckets (≤3 wk/quarter, rate-limited, logged, displayed) excluded.
y(e,b) = log1p(pr_days(e,b))     -- pr_days = distinct days with ≥1 merged PR
   -- THE LOC TERM IS DELETED at launch: y was LOC-dominated (σ≈1.0 vs 0.48) — the headline's output
   -- leg was a lines trend, and the additions-only reading would have fired "Drifting" at the org's
   -- best refactorers (S8). LOC returns only path-filtered at 0.5 weight post-acq #2, validation-gated.
   -- pr_days (not PR count): same-day salami-slicing adds nothing (A7). Bucketing by MERGED date v0,
   -- switching to OPENED date when opened_at lands — merge-timing banking then moves nothing (A4).
   -- non-PR profile: M_out := NOT COMPUTABLE. (The old rule substituted the practice index for y,
   -- making the entire headline synthesizable from self-generated telemetry — the ghost-profile
   -- attack, A1. No substitution survives.)
M_out_raw = TheilSen(y); SE via moving-block bootstrap (block=2 buckets, B=1000)
M_prac    = same machinery on biweekly meaningful-day counts
ZERO-OUT (BEFORE the state machine): slope := 0 iff |slope| < max(10%/quarter, 1×SE)
   -- fixes the order bug where +0.0001 noise slopes read "Improving" (S1)
SPARSE RULE: if >50% of pairwise Theil–Sen slopes are exactly 0 (tie-degenerate), M_out := "too
   sparse to trend" — an unmeasured state, never fed to any gate. (At λ=0.3 PRs/wk, 62% of engineers
   have a deterministic zero slope; live detrending then manufactured "Drifting" for them, S2.)
COHORT DRIFT (context only, NEVER a gate): D(c) = Theil–Sen slope of the cohort's MEDIAN bucket
   series (dense even when individuals are sparse), FROZEN prior quarter, published.
   -- The old live median-of-individual-slopes was a live peer statistic in the headline — the
   -- victim/cartel channel the design claimed to have deleted (F2/A6). Gating on the raw slope
   -- accepts mild drift-inflated "Improving" as the lesser harm at self-view stakes.
CEILING WAIVER: if mean meaningful days/active week ≥ ⌈5·FTE⌉, M_prac>0 is treated as satisfied
   ("Sustained") — the old machine structurally denied its top state to its best users (S11/A3).
Regime resets truncate the baseline on: HR team/function/level change; FTE-fraction change (F16);
   ≥4 consecutive away weeks; or a SELF-SERVICE "my role changed" reset (≤2/year, logged — the
   IC→de-facto-lead transition changes no HR field, F6) → "RECALIBRATING (k/9 buckets)".
   "Improving" is SUPPRESSED for the first window after any reset (resets were a scheduled sandbag
   opportunity, A4). New hires: "Drifting" suppressed 24 weeks post-hire (the onboarding ramp
   otherwise guarantees a flattering "Improving" then a near-certain "Drifting" at months 4–6, F17).
   Interns: descriptive profile only (program shorter than the window), declared upfront (F18).
```

**The headline state machine (evaluation order is normative):**
```
1 usable buckets < 9                      → "Building baseline (k of 9)"
2 post-reset first window                 → "Recalibrating" (Improving suppressed)
3 Q_state = attention (any repo)          → "Mixed — check quality flags"
4 M_out too sparse / not computable:
    M_prac > 0                            → "Practice trend up — output unmeasured"
    else                                  → "Steady (output unmeasured)"
5 ceiling waiver AND M_out ≥ 0            → "Sustained — high practice, output steady"
6 M_prac > 0 AND M_out ≥ 0 AND CORROBORATION → "Improving"
    CORROBORATION: ≥5 checked PRs in-window (Q evidence exists) OR Flow band ≥ prior quarter's.
    If corroboration fails: "Improving (unverified — quality evidence too thin)".
    -- "Improving" must not be reachable from self-generated telemetry alone (A3/A5)
7 M_out > 0 AND M_prac ≤ 0                → "Steady (output up)"   ← volume gate: output volume
                                                                     alone never yields "Improving"
8 M_out confidently negative (CI excludes 0 AND beyond the zero-out floor; suppressed per the
  post-hire rule)                         → "Drifting — output trending down"
    -- REDEFINED (F4): practice-down/output-steady no longer lands here. A senior who reduces AI
    -- usage while output holds — METR's central finding for experienced devs — reads "Steady"
    -- with a neutral note ("practice depth declined; output held — if intentional, no action needed").
9 else                                    → "Steady"
Bootstrap P(slope>0) is displayed as graded language ("more likely rising than not — 68%").
```
The heavy-vs-light-week uplift contrast (Δ) from the donor designs is **not computed** — deleted by unanimous judge consensus.

**Facet E — Experience pulse (quarterly, ~9 items; phase 2).** Friction/experience **facts only, never self-estimated time savings** (METR's ~40pp miscalibration): share of AI-assisted changes needing major rework, friction sources, tool fit; agent builders additionally self-report who consumes their agents (CHAOSS structured-self-report pattern [primary]) — rendered as a labeled self-reported consumers list (voice, not score). Displayed vs anonymous function-cohort bands. Non-response = "no data", never scored. Satisfies SPACE's ≥1-perceptual-dimension rule.

**Cost Awareness strip — displayed, NEVER scored.** Own 13-week spend sparkline vs own 26-week baseline + tool-category mix + anonymous cohort IQR; **agent-platform engineers are contextualized against agent-platform users' IQR, not their function cohort's** (agentic workflows are structural token outliers, F8); high-spend periods framed "investment phase", never "wasteful" (spend ≠ difficulty ≠ waste — 30x same-task variance [independent]; per-dollar ranks invert productivity [vendor]; FinOps showback stops above the individual [primary]).

**Validation loop (what stops this being a vanity adoption score).** Quarterly, per function×level (hierarchically pooled, offline): for engineers with a sustained transition (≥8 weeks at a new Practice stage or Flow band), within-person 12w-before/after deltas on merged-PR trend, PR size/share>p90, CI-anomaly rate, (v1) retouch/revert. Effect + CI shown on the dashboard ("evidence: moderate, n=214 transitions"). **Demotion rule, rebuilt post red-team:** fires only on **evidence of absence** — the pooled CI must *exclude* the pre-registered minimum effect for 2 consecutive quarters (equivalence logic); failure-to-reject never demotes (the old null-or-negative rule was an underpowered test whose default outcome was demotion: at n=10 transitions/quarter, a genuinely working rung showed "2 consecutive nulls" with ~65% probability, S4). Joint success criteria pre-registered per rung (the old rule's own advertised success mode — "smaller PRs at unchanged throughput" — satisfied its demotion condition). The loop publishes its own power beside every verdict; an **outcome-coverage gate** keeps the demotion path live only in cohorts whose work mode the outcome set covers (F9); transition cohorts are canary-screened (A10). Outcomes never enter any individual's score (DORA 2024: individual gains and system outcomes diverge [primary]). The loop also reports the empirical P–M_prac correlation (they share a data series; the design does not claim facet orthogonality, S11) and the headline's week-over-week concordance.

**Integrity canaries (org-side aggregates, never per-person, never a gate):** (1) PR-size pile-up under band edges; (2) falling CI-checked share / falling pr_weeks; (3) near-floor session-token distributions / near-zero daily-token variance; (4) retouch-lag bunching past day 21; (5) agent runs with ~zero tokens; (6) **timing entropy** (cron signatures — humans are irregular, A2); (7) same-day multi-merge bursts to one repo (salami slicing, A7); (8) band-edge pile-up in F shares; (9) reciprocal-revert pairs (once linkage lands); (10) self-marked on-call week rates; (11) `class_availability` rows that never change.

### 6.3 Why it wins, and how it answers the brief's hard limits

**Vs the current metric's 7 problems:** (1) CI demoted from multiplier to a per-repo, minimum-evidence, outlier-boundary flag that can only cap; (2) no denominator anywhere — all tools incl. the agent platform feed Practice; agent engineers get scored practice + honest "unmeasured" output states, upgraded to a true output facet when caller identity lands; (3) no ratios, no live percentiles — rule ladders, frozen blended bands, a self-vs-self raw-slope headline; (4) all AND-gates deleted — labeled states, EB shrinkage, displayed intervals; (5) spend never divides anything (the Jellyfish inversion is impossible by construction); (6) function×level precision-weighted cohorts for context, per-repo signal normalization, score-neutral work-mode profiles; (7) the <3-PR ⇒ 1.0 default is abolished — low evidence yields "not computable" with evidence-labeled states, and (post-S3) more evidence no longer increases false-flag exposure.

**Vs the hard limits:** *no AI-vs-human attribution* → no facet claims to isolate the AI's contribution; nothing breaks if attribution never arrives. *No task difficulty/value* → no cross-person volume verdict exists: Flow is a discipline share, Momentum is self-relative and volume-gated, bands are frozen context, spend is unscored — a hard, slow, expensive quarter reads "Steady" with an investment-phase cost strip. *No review depth/defect linkage* → quality is an anomaly floor; the real counterweights are metadata-only acquisitions; truth-testing lives in the cohort validation loop, not per-person defect claims. *No agent-platform output signal* → first-class practice scoring; "unmeasured — we don't score what we can't see"; the caller-identity acquisition is a **GA precondition** for this profile (F8), so the interim has an owner and an end. *~2-week data lag* → per-source watermarks; "data pending" weeks excluded both sides; per-facet watermark dates displayed. *Strictly self-view* → no live peer statistic exists anywhere, including detrending (frozen prior-quarter constant); cohort context is frozen, anonymous, aggregate; canaries never gate an individual.

### 6.5 Implementation sketch — HAVE data only

**Warehouse tables:** `usage_daily(user_id, day, tool_id, dollars, tokens, sessions, active_day, runs)` [HAVE]; `prs(user_id, pr_id, repo_id*, merged_at*, lines_changed)` [HAVE; *verify repo_id + merge timestamps]; `ci(pr_id, checked, clean)` [HAVE]; `hr(user_id, function, level, team, fte_fraction, effective_from)` [HAVE; verify FTE exposure]; NEW-trivial config: `tool_class`, `class_availability`, `seat_assignment`; derived frozen-blended snapshots: `tau`, `repo_band`, `f_cutpoints`, `cohort_drift`, `watermark`; `self_events(user_id, kind∈{role_change_reset, oncall_week, q_annotation}, week, note)`.

**Weekly batch (per engineer):** (1) resolve per-source watermarks → AS_OF; build the zero-filled 26-week spine; mark active/data-pending weeks; compute meaningful days per class against day-grain τ_c. (2) Graded C1, seat-dated C2, C3, active-week C4, FTE-scaled → stage; hysteresis on the 4 newest weeks; Stage-4 external-event check. (3) Join PRs to `repo_band` (min-evidence grain) → Beta posterior → posterior-probability Flow band. (4) Q per repo at monthly frozen boundaries, min n=10, outlier-boundary Wilson test → flags + annotations. (5) Biweekly pr_days series (on-call/pending buckets excluded) → Theil–Sen + block bootstrap → zero-out → sparse check → frozen `cohort_drift` context → regime-reset check → headline state machine in normative order. (6) Emit one row per engineer per facet: {value, band/stage/state, interval, because-facts, watermark_date}.

Core pseudo-SQL (Practice + Momentum inputs) is in `RECOMMENDED_DESIGN.md` §5.3; everything is joins, window functions, and one small Python batch step (Theil–Sen + bootstrap). No MCMC anywhere.

**Phased rollout + pilot gates:** Phase 1 (weeks 1–4, SQL-only): Practice ladder, config tables, watermarks, cost strip, profiles, unmeasured panels, canaries. Phase 2 (quarter 1): Flow bands, Q outlier floor, Momentum + headline, hysteresis, validation-loop harness with pre-registered equivalence rules, sensitivity analyses. Phase 3 (as acquired): opened_at bucketing; durability netting; Experience survey; reviewer-credit and agent-consumer facets; agent-platform profile GA (gated on acquisition #1). **Pilot gates (committed):** (i) measure headline week-over-week concordance on identical-behavior engineers — **if <0.8, coarsen the state vocabulary until it clears** (better honest-coarse than fake-fine); (ii) report the empirical P–M_prac correlation; (iii) run the threshold sensitivity analysis before org-wide launch.

### 6.7 Honest residual weaknesses (stated in-product where relevant)

1. **Construct retreat, stated plainly:** ALP measures practice + trajectory, not amplification. Nobody can measure per-person amplification under these limits; any design claiming to is overclaiming. The dashboard keeps saying so.
2. **The direction-of-practice bet is unproven.** Practice→outcomes evidence is associational (DORA capabilities) and METR cuts against it for seniors. The validation loop tests the bet under equivalence logic, but is observational and self-selected, and in small cohorts it will say "cannot validate" for years. If rungs keep failing validation, the committed demotion path degrades Practice to a labeled descriptive profile.
3. **The quality floor is mostly promissory at launch** — a deliberately conservative CI outlier flag until acquisitions #2/#4 land; shown in-product as "quality coverage: partial." Post-S3 it will fire rarely, *by design*: a floor that rarely binds is the honest price of not running a false-flag lottery.
4. **Invisible work stays invisible.** Review, mentoring, design, incident response, unmerged experimentation, and (until #1) agent outputs are acknowledged, not measured. Agent-platform engineers get a thinner profile in the interim — now with a GA gate so the interim has an owner and an end.
5. **Practice theater residual** — bounded to the Practice stage (a private label), canaried (including timing entropy), excluded from the headline by the corroboration gate, and no longer misrepresented as defended by τ_c. An alternate-quarter ramp/coast sawtooth survives; its payoff is one private label per cycle, and coast quarters no longer read "Drifting," so the cycle's incentive is weak.
6. **Motivation risk and feedback latency:** for many engineers most quarters the headline will read "Steady," and real behavior change takes ~3 weeks to reach facets and ~2 months to reach the trajectory (stated in-UI). "Drifting" fires only on confident evidence, so modest real declines often read "Steady" — deliberate; the alternative is showing noise. Engagement load falls on facet levers and evidence notes, not score movement.
7. **Ceiling effect:** in a mandate-driven org most engineers may reach Stage 3 within ~a year; the "Sustained" state gives at-ceiling engineers a headline to keep, but a saturated ladder with a stalled refresh becomes a hygiene checkbox. The refresh path (validation-driven rung revision; reviewer-credit and agent-consumer facets) must actually be exercised.
8. **Boundary and edge residuals:** true-borderline engineers still see some stage/band noise (disclosed via the boundary-distance cue); published cutpoints invite edge camping (watched by canaries, accepted as the price of interpretability); F-side cosmetic PR splitting is unfixable without diff content; tool_class classification remains a lobbyable human decision (bounded by published criteria and a rationale log); self-marked on-call weeks are self-report (rate-limited, displayed, canaried, replaceable by rotation data).
9. **Cognitive compression risk:** five facets invite mental re-averaging into one number; framing and the absence of any numeric composite mitigate, not prevent.
10. **No deployed precedent for the assembly.** Every component has an evidence-backed track record; the assembly carries three adversarial passes and 44 logged dispositions — but ALP as a whole is unvalidated. Pilot with the validation loop armed and the concordance gate enforced from day one is mandatory.

## 8. Open problems — honestly flagged dead ends

1. **Per-person leverage-as-amplification is unmeasurable under these constraints — full stop.** It is a causal, within-person counterfactual; without attribution, task difficulty, or randomization, no cross-sectional number can be it. The world's best measurement attempts are themselves degrading: METR's 2026 update reports 30–50% of developers refusing to work without AI, calling its own data "an unreliable signal" [independent]. Any dashboard that names a number "leverage" without the practice+trajectory reframing is overclaiming. Task-time RCTs remain valid only as *org-level validation* instruments (staggered rollouts à la Cui), never as a per-engineer metric.
2. **No statistical transform rescues CI-clean-rate.** Shrinkage on a saturated signal converges every posterior to the prior. A "first-attempt CI pass" event would restore some signal but is a NEW acquisition of uncertain value given iterate-to-green happens pre-push too. CI survives only as an anomaly floor.
3. **Agent-platform output value has no industry answer yet.** OTel GenAI has no success semantics; Langfuse-style success is builder-defined (owner-controlled, never comparable across people); business-value baselines per workflow will never exist per-engineer. Adoption-by-others (signal #1) is the best available proxy and it is still gameable at the margin (astroturf rings) and unfair to system-invoked automations — agents whose consumers are machines/pipelines stay unmeasured. Say so; don't fake it.
4. **Invisible work stays invisible.** Review, mentoring, design, incident response, unmerged ML experimentation: the invisible-labor literature diagnoses this thoroughly and offers no telemetry cure. ALP's honesty panel and the review-metadata acquisition narrow, but do not close, the gap. The Dan-North "Tim" — zero individual output, team made faster — remains unmeasurable in any output-shaped system.
5. **The timidity trap has no data-side fix here.** Durability/quality signals reward unambitious work; with no task-difficulty signal, the design cannot distinguish safe-and-durable from bold-and-worth-it. Mitigation is framing (flags as prompts, never deductions) — a mitigation, not a solution.
6. **Dollar non-stationarity.** Token prices fell ~90% since 2023; Copilot moved to consumption credits mid-2026; vendor subsidies are unwound unpredictably [independent/vendor]. Any spend display (even unscored showback) is not comparable across quarters without deflation — use gross metered consumption, tokens as a shadow axis, and quarterly re-anchoring; accept the residual.
7. **Ambient workflow drift contaminates all self-trends.** As org-wide AI defaults inflate PR cadence and verbosity, a raw self-vs-self "Improving" partially reflects the calendar. ALP shows frozen cohort drift as context and accepts mild drift-inflation as the lesser harm vs live detrending's victim channel — a trade-off, not a fix.
8. **The headline's reliability is a hypothesis until piloted.** Composed error analysis puts week-over-week concordance around 0.4–0.6 pre-patch; the committed pilot gate (≥0.8 or coarsen the vocabulary) is the honest response, and it may end with a 2-state headline ("on track"/"check flags"). A sentence is not automatically more reliable than a number.
9. **The validation loop will say "cannot validate" for years in small cohorts.** Equivalence-logic demotion needs power that L8-SRE-sized cells won't have; the loop publishes its own power instead of borrowing significance. The practice→outcomes bet stays partially unproven for those cohorts indefinitely.
10. **Practice theater is bounded, not eliminated.** A scripted session habit can still lift the Practice stage (a private label, canaried, excluded from the headline by the corroboration gate). The ambient weak form — "open the tool twice a week" — is indistinguishable from habitual adoption and is accepted at self-view stakes.
11. **Ceiling effect / product half-life.** In a mandate-driven org most engineers may reach Stage 3 within ~a year; a saturated ladder with a stalled refresh becomes a hygiene checkbox. The refresh path (validation-driven rung revision; reviewer-credit and agent-consumer facets) is designed in but must actually be exercised.
12. **No deployed precedent for the assembly.** Every component has an evidence-backed track record and the assembly carries three adversarial passes with 44 logged dispositions — but ALP as a whole is unvalidated, exactly like the metric it replaces was.
